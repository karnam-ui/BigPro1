1.0 PREAMBLE:
1.1 VNR Vignana Jyothi Institute of Engineering and Technology (VNRVJIET) is committed
to achieving academic excellence through innovative teaching-learning processes,
continuous curriculum enhancement and robust evaluation mechanisms.
1.2 In line with the guidelines of the University Grants Commission (UGC), All India Council
for Technical Education (AICTE) and Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University
Hyderabad (JNTUH), the Institute introduced the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS)
for all Undergraduate and Postgraduate Programmes from the academic year 2015-
2016 and continues its implementation under the Academic Regulations stipulated
herein.
1.3 The implementation of the CBCS at VNRVJIET is intended to align with national
initiatives for standardization of higher education and to enhance student flexibility,
academic mobility, interdisciplinary learning and skill development.
1.4 In consonance with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, the Institute has
incorporated several academic reforms and curricular initiatives into these Academic
Regulations and Curriculum, including but not limited to:
i. Mandatory Internship
ii. Mandatory Innovation and Entrepreneurship Course
iii. Mandatory Courses on Environmental Science, Happiness and Wellbeing and
Intellectual Property Rights

and interdisciplinary learning and prepare graduates to meet global, ethical and
societal challenges, while upholding the Institute’s commitment to academic quality
and excellence.
2.0 SHORT TITLE, APPLICATION AND COMMENCEMENT:
2.1 These Regulations shall be called the “VNRVJIET B.Tech. Choice Based Credit System
(CBCS) Academic Regulations, 2025” and may be referred to as the “VNRVJIET B.Tech.
CBCS R25 Regulations”.
2.2 These Regulations shall apply to all B.Tech. Regular (Full-Time) Undergraduate
Programmes offered by the Institute.
2.3 These Regulations shall come into force for all student batches admitted from the
academic year 2025-2026 onwards.
2.4 These Regulations are comprehensive and include, inter alia, provisions relating to the
definitions of key terms, the semester system, the credit system, the grading system,
Programme Structure, the Scheme of Examinations and Evaluation procedures.
2.5 The Institute may, from time to time, revise, amend, or update these Regulations, the
scheme of examinations and the syllabi, based on the recommendations of the
respective Boards of Studies (BoS), subject to the approval of the Academic Council.
2.6 Any such revisions, amendments, or updates shall take effect from the date specified
and shall be applicable to such batches of students, including those already
undergoing the programme, as approved by the Academic Council.
3.0 CHOICE BASED CREDIT SYSTEM:
3.1 The Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) is an academic framework that enables a
student to qualify for the award of a degree by earning the prescribed number of
credits as specified in the Programme Curriculum. The credits earned represent the
academic workload and learning effort associated with a course.
3.2 Each course is assigned a fixed number of credits based on its depth, breadth and
intended learning outcomes. Student performance in each course is evaluated through
a grading system and the grade points obtained reflect the level of proficiency
achieved.
3.3 The CBCS offers the following features and advantages:
i. Flexibility in course selection: Students may choose elective courses from a broad
spectrum offered by various departments.
ii. Opportunities for additional learning: Students may register for courses beyond
the minimum prescribed requirements to enhance their knowledge and skills.
iii. Interdisciplinary learning: Students may adopt an interdisciplinary approach by
selecting courses across different domains.
iv. Academic mobility: Credits earned from other departments or recognized higher
education institutions in India may be transferred, subject to approval as per
Institute norms.
2
v. Utilization of faculty expertise: Students can benefit from specialized courses
offered by faculty members with domain expertise.
3.4 The CBCS permits flexibility in completing the programme of study within the
maximum duration prescribed under these Regulations.

7.0 ELIGIBILITY FOR ADMISSION:
7.1 The eligibility criteria for admission to the B.Tech. Degree Programmes offered at
VNRVJIET shall be as prescribed by the Government of Telangana and in accordance
with the statutory provisions in force.
7.2 Unless otherwise notified by the Government of Telangana, the criteria shall be as
follows:
a) The candidate shall be an Indian National or a Non-Resident Indian (NRI).
b) The candidate shall have completed sixteen (16) years of age as on 31 December of
the Academic Year in which admission is sought.
c) The candidate shall have passed the qualifying examination (10+2 or equivalent) as
on the date of admission, as recognized by the Board of Intermediate Education (BIE),
Telangana State.
7.3 The seats in each programme of the Institute shall be classified into the following
categories, in accordance with the Government Orders in force:
i) Category-A Seats:
These shall be filled through the centralized admission counselling process, based on
the rank secured by the candidate in the Engineering, Agriculture and Pharmacy
Common Entrance Test (EAPCET) conducted by the Government of Telangana.
ii) Category-B Seats:
These shall be filled by the Institute in accordance with the Government Orders issued
by the Government of Telangana from time to time.
7.4 Direct Admission to Second Year (Lateral Entry Scheme):
A candidate shall be eligible for admission directly into the III Semester (II Year I
Semester) of the B.Tech. Degree Programme under the Lateral Entry Scheme, based on
the rank secured in the Engineering Common Entrance Test [ECET (for Diploma
Holders)] conducted by the Government of Telangana and subject to compliance with
the other admission criteria prescribed in the Government Orders in force.
8.0 MEDIUM OF INSTRUCTION:
8.1 The medium of instruction for all B.Tech. Degree Programmes shall be English.
8.2 This shall apply to:
i. All courses prescribed in the Programme Curriculum.
ii. Content delivery in lectures, laboratories and tutorials.
iii. Examinations, assessments and evaluations.
iv. Seminars, presentations and project evaluations.
9.0 B.TECH. PROGRAMME STRUCTURE:
9.1 The structure of the B.Tech. Degree Programmes offered at VNRVJIET shall be in
accordance with the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) and the Credit Based Semester
System (CBSS), as defined by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and aligned with
the Model Curriculum prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education
(AICTE) and Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Hyderabad (JNTUH).
9.2 The total credits prescribed for B.Tech. Degree Programme shall be 164, out of which
a student shall earn a minimum of 160 credits for the award of the B.Tech. Degree.
9.3 Semester Scheme:
9.3.1 The B.Tech. Degree Programmes shall follow the semester scheme with the following
provisions:
12
9.3.1.1 Each academic year shall consist of two (2) semesters, namely Odd and Even semesters,
sequentially numbered from I to VIII Semester.
9.3.1.2 Each semester shall comprise seventeen (17) weeks, excluding the Sessional
Examinations and Semester End Examinations.
9.3.1.3 Each semester shall comprise Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) and Semester End
Examination (SEE) components, as prescribed in these Regulations.
10.0 DURATION OF THE ACADEMIC PROGRAMMES:
10.1 The normal duration of the B.Tech. Degree Programme shall be as follows:
i. Four (4) academic years for students admitted to the I Year of the programme.
ii. Three (3) academic years for students admitted under the Lateral Entry Scheme
(LES), i.e., Diploma Holders admitted directly into the III Semester (II Year I
Semester).
10.2 The maximum permissible duration for completion of the programme shall be twice
the normal duration, as given below:
i. Eight (8) academic years for students admitted to the I Year.
ii. Six (6) academic years for students admitted under the Lateral Entry Scheme (LES).

12.0 MANDATORY INDUCTION PROGRAMME:
12.1 An Induction Programme of one (1) week duration shall be conducted for all students
admitted into B.Tech. Degree Programmes, at the commencement of the I Semester.
This programme shall be a non-credit mandatory audit course.
12.2 Regular instructional classes shall commence only after the completion of the Induction
Programme.
12.3 The objectives of the Induction Programme shall include:
i. Facilitating a smooth transition from school education to the professional academic
environment.
ii. Familiarizing students with the academic structure, regulations and facilities of the
Institute.
iii. Encouraging interpersonal bonding and team-building among students.

13.0 COURSE REGISTRATION:

based on individual competence, prerequisites and interests.

Campus Automation Management System (CAMS) within the dates specified in the
Academic Calendar, after obtaining approval from the Faculty Advisor/Mentor and the
Head of the Department.

fulfilment of prerequisites (if any) and payment of prescribed tuition fees and all dues,
including Institute and hostel dues.

the earliest valid submission recorded in the system shall be treated as final.

13.1.5 Course choices exercised during online registration are final and cannot be changed
thereafter.
13.1.6 However, if a course listed for offering in that semester could not be offered due to
unavoidable circumstances, the Head of the Department may permit suitable
alternative course options and shall notify the students through a time-bound schedule
before commencement of classwork.
13.1.7 No course registration shall be permitted beyond the notified schedule.

complete course registration using their own credentials.

that is equivalent in content to any course prescribed in their Programme Curriculum.
13.2.3 Students shall not register for an Open Elective that has already been
completed/credited earlier, or that is equivalent to any course to be studied in
subsequent semesters.

13.3.1 Students shall register for six Professional Elective (PE) courses (PE-I to PE-VI) from the
baskets specified in the Programme Structure.
13.3.2 Students may choose Professional Electives from within their discipline or opt for
interdisciplinary Professional Electives offered by other departments, subject to
fulfillment of prerequisites or waiver approved by the Dean of Academics.
13.3.3 One Professional Elective shall be pursued mandatorily through the SWAYAM-NPTEL
MOOC platform, as specified in the Programme Structure and students must
successfully earn the corresponding credit as prescribed in these Academic
Regulations.
13.3.4 Students may register for additional equivalent MOOCs approved by the Institute in
lieu of the remaining Professional Electives (other than the mandatory MOOC
mentioned in 13.3.3), based on the list notified from time to time.

13.4.1 Students shall be permitted to register for approved equivalent Professional Elective
MOOCs only through the SWAYAM-NPTEL platform, as per the Programme Structure

registered under MOOCs in V Semester (III Year I Semester).
13.4.3 Credits earned through MOOCs registered one semester in advance shall be submitted
in the subsequent semester for assessment and credit transfer.
13.4.4 Students who fail to secure a pass grade in a registered MOOC shall register for the

Minors/Honors courses, as per these Regulations and approved by the Institute.

14.0 ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS AND DETENTION POLICY:
14.1 Students shall be eligible to appear for the Semester End Examinations (SEE) only if
they secure a minimum of 75% aggregate attendance in all registered courses of the
semester. Aggregate attendance refers to the combined attendance percentage across
all courses registered in the semester.
14.2 Condonation of Attendance: A shortage of aggregate attendance between 65% and
75% may be condoned by the Institute Academic Committee on genuine and valid
grounds and upon payment of the prescribed condonation fee as notified from time
to time.
14.3 Attendance credit equivalent to two lecture periods per theory course shall be given if
the student appears for the Sessional Examination of that course.
14.4 Attendance below 65% shall not be condoned under any circumstances.
14.5 Students whose shortage of attendance is not condoned shall be detained. Their
semester registration shall be cancelled, including CIE marks and they shall not be
permitted to appear for the SEE or be promoted to the next semester, notwithstanding
the promotion provisions under Clause 24.0.
14.6 Detained student(s) shall seek re-admission into the same semester when it is offered
in the subsequent academic year. The Academic Regulations in force at the time of re-
admission shall apply.
14.7 If the prescribed Professional/Open Electives are not offered in the semester of re-
admission, students shall register for approved alternative electives from the same
category, as notified by the department.
14.8 Students under disciplinary suspension shall not be permitted to appear for the SEE.
14.9 A student who has already fulfilled the attendance requirement of a semester shall not
be eligible for re-admission to the same semester.

16.0 DISTRIBUTION OF MARKS AND EVALUATION:
16.1 General:
16.1.1 The performance of a student in every registered course, including Theory,
Practical/Laboratory, Engineering Drawing/Graphics/Design, Skill Development
Courses, Value Added Courses, Field Based Research Project, Internship/Industry
Oriented Mini-Project, Design Thinking and Major Project, shall be evaluated for a total
of 100 marks.
16.1.2 For all courses, except those otherwise specified, the evaluation pattern shall consist
of:
(a) Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE): 40 Marks
(b) Semester End Examination (SEE): 60 Marks
16.2 Theory Courses:
16.2.1 Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE):
16.2.1.1 Each Theory course shall carry forty (40) marks for Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE).
16.2.1.2 The CIE shall consist of:
• Sessional Examinations (SE): 30 marks
• Class Assessment (CA): 5 marks
• Experiential Learning Assessment (ELA): 5 marks
16.2.1.3 Two Sessional Examinations, designated as SE-I and SE-II, each of two (2) hours
duration and carrying thirty (30) marks, shall be conducted as per the Academic
Calendar.
16.2.1.4 The syllabus coverage for SE-I shall include Unit I, Unit II and first half of Unit III whereas
for SE-II shall include second half of Unit III, Unit IV and Unit V.
16.2.1.5 The question paper pattern for Sessional Examinations shall be as follows:
i) Part-A: 5Q x 2M = 10M
• Five (5) compulsory short-answer questions of 2 marks each.
• Two questions each from Unit-I and Unit-II and one question from the first half of
Unit-III in First Sessional Examination (SE-I)
• One question from the second half of Unit-III and 2 questions each from Unit-IV
and Unit-V in Second Sessional Examination (SE-II)
ii) Part-B: 20M
• Six descriptive questions, i.e., two questions from each unit, with internal choice i.e.,
‘either’ ‘or’ choice.
• The student shall answer one question from each unit.
• A maximum of two sub-divisions i.e. (a) and/or (b) shall be permitted in a question.
a) Sessional Examination-I (SE-I):
• Unit I: Q.1 OR Q.2 8 marks
• Unit II: Q.3 OR Q.4 8 marks
• Unit III: Q.5 OR Q.6 4 marks
21
b) Sessional Examination-II (SE-II):
• Unit III: Q.1 OR Q.2 4 marks
• Unit IV: Q.3 OR Q.4 8 marks
• Unit V: Q.5 OR Q.6 8 marks
iii) The average of SE-I and SE-II marks shall constitute the final Sessional Examination
score.
16.2.1.6 Class Assessment (CA) shall be conducted twice during the semester as per the
Academic Calendar using Alternative Assessment Tools (AAT), such as assignments,
quizzes, online tests, or objective examinations. The average of the two assessments
shall constitute five (5) marks for CA.
16.2.1.7 Experiential Learning Assessment (ELA) shall be conducted twice during the semester
and may include AAT such as course projects, viva-voce, poster presentations, case
studies, open-ended problems, simulations, or computational assignments. The
average of the two ELAs shall constitute five (5) marks for ELA.
16.2.1.8 The total CIE marks i.e., forty (40 marks) for Theory Courses shall be the sum of:
• Average of the Sessional Examinations
• Average of the Class Assessment and
• Average of the Experiential Learning Assessment
16.2.2 Computer-Based Test (CBT):
16.2.2.1 A Computer-Based Test (CBT) shall be provided in each theory course as an additional
internal assessment mechanism under specified conditions.
16.2.2.2 A student shall be eligible to appear for the CBT in a course if the student has:
(a) missed one of the two Sessional Examinations due to genuine medical reasons or
other unavoidable circumstances, supported by valid documentary evidence and
prior approval of the Principal, or
(b) appeared in both the Sessional Examinations but wishes to improve the internal
marks obtained in the course.
16.2.2.3 The CBT shall be conducted at the end of the semester for one (1) hour duration,
carrying a maximum of thirty (30) marks, covering the entire syllabus prescribed for the
course.
16.2.2.4 The CBT shall consist of thirty (30) objective-type multiple-choice questions (MCQs),
each carrying one (1) mark, with four (4) answer options and only one correct answer.
There shall be no negative marking for incorrect answers.
16.2.2.5 Questions in the CBT shall be uniformly distributed across all five units of the syllabus
and shall be framed as per Bloom’s Taxonomy levels as follows:
• Remember (Q1-Q10)
• Understand (Q11-Q20) and
• Apply (Q21-Q30).
16.2.2.6 The marks obtained in the CBT shall be treated as equivalent to the marks obtained in
one Sessional Examination for the purpose of computation of internal marks.
16.2.2.7 For the calculation of Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) marks in a course, the
average of the best two scores obtained from the three assessments, namely the two
Sessional Examinations and the CBT, together with the other prescribed internal
assessment components, shall be considered.
16.2.2.8 A student who remains absent for a Sessional Examination shall be awarded
zero (0) marks for that examination for internal assessment purposes.

16.2.3.1 The Semester End Examination (SEE) for Theory Courses shall be conducted for a

• Five (5) compulsory short-answer questions, one from each unit of the syllabus

from each unit with internal choice, i.e., ‘either’ ‘or’ choice.

a) Prerequisite Courses: To provide foundational knowledge and the necessary
background required for successful completion of regular courses.
b) Reinforcement Courses: To strengthen and broaden understanding of topics
covered in regular courses through alternative perspectives.
c) Aspirational Courses: To facilitate advanced exposure and next-level learning beyond
the prescribed curriculum.
16.9.4 MOOCs may be pursued by students based on their academic interests and learning
needs, subject to:
a) relevance of the course to the programme of study;
b) availability of the MOOCs during the concerned semester; and
c) prior approval for considering such courses as recommended learning resources.
16.9.5 MOOCs and additional learning resources introduced under these Regulations are
intended to promote self-learning, foster lifelong learning habits and enhance student
exposure to global academic content, in alignment with UGC guidelines.
16.10 Value Added Courses:
16.10.1 The evaluation pattern for Value Added Courses shall be the same as that prescribed
for theory courses under these Regulations.
16.10.2 The Sessional Examinations and Semester End Examinations for Value Added Courses
shall be scheduled independently and shall not be combined with, or overlap, the
mainstream examinations of the regular curriculum.
16.10.3 A proctored Sessional Examination of one-hour or 45 minutes duration shall be
conducted during regular class hours by the respective course instructor. The conduct
of this examination shall not disrupt other scheduled classes on that day.
16.10.4 The Semester End Examinations for Value Added Courses shall be scheduled and
notified by the Examination Branch from time to time, as per institutional requirements.

16.11 Skill Development Courses:
16.11.1 The Curriculum shall include four (4) Skill Development Courses, to be offered in the III
Semester (II Year I Semester), IV Semester (II Year II Semester), V Semester (III Year I
Semester) and VI Semester (III Year II Semester) of the B.Tech. Programme.
16.11.2 Each Skill Development Course shall carry one (1) credit and shall be treated as a
distinct academic requirement within the Programme Structure.
16.11.3 The evaluation pattern for Skill Development Courses shall be identical to that
prescribed for laboratory courses, comprising Continuous Internal Evaluation and
Semester End Examination, conducted by Internal and External examiner, as applicable.
16.11.4 Skill Development Courses are intended to enhance cognitive and psychomotor skills,
thereby strengthening students’ practical, analytical and application-oriented
competencies.
16.11.5 Departments shall ensure that the Skill Development Courses offered are aligned with
the expected graduate attributes and contribute meaningfully to the overall
Programme Outcomes.
16.12 Mandatory Audit Courses:
16.12.1 A student shall pursue all mandatory audit courses as specified in the approved course
structure of the B.Tech. Programme.
16.12.2 Mandatory Audit Courses shall be classified as compulsory requirements, but shall not
carry any credits.
16.12.3 Although Mandatory Audit Courses do not carry credits, a student must secure a
minimum of 40 marks out of 100 to obtain a “Satisfactory” status, which is a mandatory
requirement for the award of the Degree.
16.12.4 Two Sessional Examinations i.e., SE-I and SE-II shall be conducted in each semester for
every Mandatory Audit Course, as per the Academic Calendar.
16.12.5 Each Sessional Examination shall be evaluated for 50 marks.
16.12.6 The question paper pattern for Sessional Examinations of Mandatory Audit Courses (50
marks each) shall be as follows:
a) Sessional Examination - I (SE-I):
• Unit I: Q.1 AND Q.2 [10 + 10 = 20 marks]
• Unit II: Q.3 AND Q.4 [10 + 10 = 20 marks]
• Unit III: Q.5 [10 marks]
b) Sessional Examination - II (SE-II):
• Unit III: Q.1 [10 marks]
• Unit IV: Q.2 AND Q.3 [10 + 10 = 20 marks]
• Unit V: Q.4 AND Q.5 [10 + 10 = 20 marks]
16.12.7 The sum of marks obtained in SE-I and SE-II shall constitute the final sessional marks
for the Mandatory Audit Course.
16.12.8 The performance in each Mandatory Audit Course shall be reported as “Satisfactory”
or “Not Satisfactory”. No numerical marks or letter grades shall be awarded.

remote or simulated operation of laboratory experiments, with the objective of
promoting curiosity, innovation and experiential learning among students.
16.13.2 The primary purpose of Virtual Laboratories is to provide students with opportunities
to perform experiments that enhance conceptual understanding and strengthen
competence in the respective disciplines.

16.13.3 Students may utilize Virtual Laboratories to perform advanced or specialized
experiments that are not available in the Institute but are relevant for supplementing
learning in specific courses.

concerned Head of the Department and communicated to the Academic Section for

16.13.5 Participation in Virtual Laboratory courses or experiments shall be optional and shall

26.0 CREDIT EXEMPTION POLICY:
26.1 Registration and Credit Requirements:
26.1.1 A student shall register for all courses prescribed in the curriculum, totaling 164 credits.
26.1.2 A student must earn a minimum of 160 credits to be eligible for the award of the
B.Tech. Degree in the concerned branch of Engineering.
26.2 Credit Exemption Provision:
26.2.1 For the purpose of satisfying the minimum credit requirement, a student may avail a
credit exemption of up to 4 credits from the total registered credits of 164.
26.2.2 The exemption permitted under Clause 26.2.1 shall apply only to courses other than:
• Professional Core Courses
• Laboratory Courses
• Seminar
• Project Work
• Field Based Research Project
• Internship/Industry Oriented Mini-Project
26.2.3 Only the best 160 credits earned by the student shall be considered for final CGPA
computation, after availing the permitted credit exemption.
26.3 Exclusion of Minor and Honors Credits:
26.3.1 Credits earned under a Minor Degree Programme shall not be counted towards the
minimum 160 credits required for the award of the regular B.Tech. Degree.
26.3.2 Credits earned under an Honors Degree Programme shall also not be counted towards
the minimum 160 credits required for the regular B.Tech. Degree.
26.4 SGPA Reporting:
26.4.1 The Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) of a semester shall be displayed on the
Grade Card only after the student has passed all courses registered in that semester.

of Engineering allotted at the time of admission if the student pursues the programme
of study for not less than four (4) academic years and not more than eight (8) academic
years from the date of commencement of the first academic year.
27.1.2 A student shall register for all courses prescribed in the curriculum corresponding to
164 credits and shall earn a minimum of 160 credits with a final CGPA of not less than
5.0.
27.1.3 A student who fails to fulfil all the academic requirements for the award of the Degree
within eight (8) academic years from the date of admission shall forfeit the seat in the
B.Tech. Programme.

29.0 HONOURS AND MINOR DEGREE PROGRAMMES:
29.1 Introduction:
29.1.1 B.Tech. Honours and Minor Degree Programmes shall be offered in all branches of the
B.Tech. Programme in accordance with the curriculum approved by the Academic
Council.
29.1.2 The Minor Degree Programme shall commence from IV Semester (II Year II Semester)
and may be pursued up to VII Semester (IV Year I Semester).
29.1.3 The Honours Degree Programme shall commence from V Semester (III Year I Semester)
and may be pursued up to VIII Semester (IV Year II Semester).
29.2 Award of B.Tech. (Honours) Degree:
29.2.1 A student shall be awarded the B.Tech. (Honours) Degree upon earning an additional
20 credits, over and above the minimum 160 credits required for the regular B.Tech.
Degree exclusively during III and IV years of the programme.
29.2.2 A minimum CGPA of 7.5 must be maintained in each semester from V to VIII, as well as
at the completion of the regular B.Tech. Degree requirements.
29.2.3 The student must pass all courses of both the regular B.Tech. Degree and the Honours
Programme in the first appearance (regular examinations).
29.2.4 Weekly instruction hours, internal evaluation, external evaluation and award of grades
for Honours courses shall be on par with the regular four-year B.Tech. Programme.
29.2.5 Credits earned in the Honours Programme shall not be transferred to the regular
B.Tech. Degree and vice-versa.
29.2.6 A student losing eligibility for any reason (including failure in any registered course of
either programme) shall not be awarded the Honours Degree but shall remain eligible
for the regular B.Tech. Degree upon satisfying its requirements.
29.2.7 Only courses listed in the approved Honours curriculum shall be permitted. Courses
already studied/registered in the regular B.Tech. Degree curriculum shall not be
repeated for Honours.
29.2.8 Prior approval of the Faculty Mentor/Advisor and Head of the Department is
mandatory before registering for the Honours Programme.
29.2.9 A student opting for the Honours Programme shall not be permitted to register for a
Minor Degree and vice-versa.
29.3 Eligibility Criteria for B.Tech. (Honours) Degree:
29.3.1 Only students entering V Semester (III Year I Semester) who have passed all courses in
the first attempt up to III Semester (II Year I Semester) with a CGPA ≥ 7.5 shall be eligible
to apply.
29.3.2 If more than 30% of students in a branch satisfy the eligibility criteria, the number of
seats shall be restricted to 30%, based on CGPA up to III Semester.
29.4 Award of B.Tech. Minor Degree:
29.4.1 A student shall be awarded a Minor Degree in a specified discipline upon earning 18
additional credits, over and above the minimum 160 credits of the regular B.Tech.
Degree, during IV to VII semesters.
38
29.4.2 The Minor Degree must be completed within the stipulated duration (twice the
duration of the Minor curriculum). Failing this, the student will not be awarded the
Minor Degree but shall remain eligible for the regular B.Tech. Degree upon fulfilling its
requirements.
29.4.3 Weekly instruction hours, evaluation procedures and grading for Minor courses shall
be identical to those of regular B.Tech. courses.
29.4.4 No transfer of credits shall be permitted between the Minor Programme and the
regular B.Tech. Degree.
29.4.5 A student may pursue only one Minor Programme. Students opting for Minor shall not
be eligible for the Honours Programme and vice-versa.
29.4.6 Courses selected for the Minor must be from the approved Minor course structure and
must not duplicate any course studied/registered in the regular B.Tech. Degree.
29.5 Eligibility Criteria for B.Tech. Minor Degree:
29.5.1 Only students entering IV Semester (II Year II Semester) with no active backlogs up to
II Semester (I Year II Semester) shall be eligible to apply for a Minor Programme.
29.5.2 If more than 50% of students in a branch satisfy the eligibility criteria, enrolment shall
be restricted to 50%, based on CGPA as per Institute norms.
29.5.3 Prior approval of the Faculty Mentor/Advisor and Head of the Department is
mandatory before registering for the Minor Programme.

MALPRACTICE RULES
DISCIPLINARY ACTION FOR IMPROPER CONDUCT IN EXAMINATIONS
A1.0 APPLICABILITY:
A1.1 These rules and the prescribed punishments shall apply to any student who indulges
in malpractice or improper conduct in any examination or evaluation process, including
internal assessments, continuous evaluation and Semester End Examinations, whether
theory, practical, project viva-voce and online or offline, conducted by the Institution.
A1.2 Wherever the term student is not explicitly mentioned in subsequent clauses, it shall
be implicitly understood to refer to the student involved in the malpractice.
A1.3 In the case of internal assessments, including sessional examinations, the punishments
described in these rules shall be applied in a similar manner and adjusted to the
seriousness of the offence, limited to the concerned course, assessment component,
or Semester, as deemed appropriate by the competent authority.
A1.4 All question papers and examination materials, whether in physical or electronic form,
shall be treated as confidential. Unauthorized access or handling of such material by
students shall constitute malpractice, irrespective of whether the examination has
commenced.
A2.0 NATURE OF MALPRACTICE AND PUNISHMENTS:
A2.1 All malpractices, including attempted, unsuccessful, or preparatory acts, such as
possession, access, or arrangements made with the intent to commit malpractice, shall
be deemed malpractice and attract punishment as prescribed for the corresponding
offence, even if detected before actual execution.
A2.2 In deciding whether a student has committed malpractice, the Malpractice Committee
will review all the evidence and determine what is most likely to have happened. The
Committee does not need absolute certainty; action may be taken if the evidence
shows that malpractice probably occurred.
A2.3 Prior to the imposition of any punishment under these rules, the student concerned
shall be given an opportunity to present their explanation before the Malpractice
Committee. The Committee shall examine the case and submit its recommendation to
the competent authority for final decision.
