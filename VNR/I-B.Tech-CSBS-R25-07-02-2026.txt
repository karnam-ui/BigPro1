UNIT-I:
Abstract Algebra: Sets, Finite sets, Power sets, Set Operations, and duality, Relations,
Types of relations, Equivalence relations, Partial Ordering, Partition of a set, Groups,
subgroups, Lagrange's theorem on finite groups (without proof). Introduction to Ring,
Integral domain and Field.
UNIT-II:
Logic: Propositional calculus - propositions and connectives, Conditional and Bi-
conditional statements, truth assignments and truth tables, validity and satisfiability,
tautology, contradiction, and contingency. Logical Equivalence and normal forms;
Algebra of propositions, Logical implication, Quantifiers, Negation of quantified
statements.
10
UNIT-III:
Recurrence Relations: Introduction, sequences, generating functions, coefficients of
generating functions, recurrence relations, solutions of homogeneous and non-
homogeneous recurrence relations using characteristic roots method.
UNIT-IV:
Boolean Algebra: Introduction of Boolean algebra, principle of duality, Basic logic
gates, truth table, Boolean expressions, canonical form, Karnaugh map.
UNIT-V:
Graph Theory: Graphs, Types of Graphs, digraphs, adjacency matrix, incidence
matrix, isomorphism, Trees, Properties of trees, Spanning trees, Minimal Spanning trees
using Kruskal’s and Prims Algorithms. Eulerian paths and circuits in graphs and
digraphs, Hamiltonian paths and circuits in graphs, Planar graphs, dual of a planer
graph, Euler’s formula, chromatic number, statement of Four-color theorem.

Basic Probability& Mathematical Expectations: Concept of experiments, sample
