of the NAAC SSR Cycle 2 information, Sensitization and Follow up quality indicators devised
during cycle, to devise a mechanism for ensuring the credibility of Academics & Admissions,
Teaching /Learning, Evaluation and Examination procedure through regular External Audit, and
strategize for Promotion of Academic, Research & Consultancy, Extension and outreach

The following are the minutes of the meeting.
i) The new NAAC SSR Format of 2017 was discussed in front of the committee. Also,
the Quality strategies devised by IQAC, how the mechanism of External Audit will
help establish the credibility of Teaching Learning, Evaluation and Examination
procedure, and the need to strategize for Promotion of a reinforced and better
Academic, Research & Consultancy, Extension and outreach activities were
discussed. The members were invited for suggestions on these points.
 Action to be taken up by the Academic Audit Cell
ii) In connection with devising a mechanism for ensuring the credibility of admissions,
one of the industry representatives said that the institute must request Govt. for the
freedom to pick up students who are good at innovative project work within the quota
of 15% NRI intake.
 Action to be taken up by PAAC
iii) Addressing the issue of Consultancy, Mr JVRS Prasad advised that the Institutes can
develop a start-up culture and train the students. He said that we have to train our
students in the new technologies to carry out innovation.
 Action to be taken up by each department
iv) Throwing light on the kind of extension and outreach activities conducted by the
institute, one of the members from CE explained the outreach activities taken up by
the Civil Engineering Department. Another idea for outreach activity was to come up
with initiatives such as Fit for Life, purpose for life initiatives from members.
 Action plan need to be prepared by each department
v) With regard to Extension and outreach activities, the external expert Mr JVRS Prasad
extended help to offer some projects to the students which can be socially useful and
relevant.
 Action to be taken up by each department
vi) A student representative expressed the need for substantial amount of time for
students to continue with the projects they have taken up and showcase it in the
project expo.
vii) The external expert, Mr. J.V.R.S. Prasad also recommended a beneficial & proactive
initiative to set up a Health, Safety & Environment (HSE) center.
viii) The meeting concluded on a positive note with the external experts wishing the
faculty and Institute to work towards excellence.

3. Initiating the agenda items the Chairman requested the Member-Secretary to place
the agenda item for discussion.
3.1 The Chairman gave a brief presentation about the Institute‟s status and
achievements.
3.2 There was a discussion about the Institute‟s placement and other Trademarks. There
were valuable suggestions and inputs towards enhancing quality from Mr.
J.V.R.S. Prasad, Dr. C. Srinagesh and Dr. D N Rao, General Secretary, VNRVJIET.
3.3 Targets such as - all the faculty members should be PhDs and all the lab
Technicians should be B.Tech graduates by 2017, have been set by Dr. DN Rao.
3.4 Capacity expansion and augmentation of infrastructure facility for all academic
departments has been discussed.
4. The Quality Manual was presented for discussion and approval.
4.1 The efforts rendered for a complete IQAC manual were appreciated.
4.2 Dr D N Rao, Ms Sandhya Kode, Dr. C. Srinagesh and Mr. J.V.R.S. Prasad agreed
that the „outcomes‟ of IQAC should be measurable and hence there is a need to
introduce metrics and rubrics as well as data, statistics etc. Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad
advised the IQAC Team to prepare a „dashboard‟ for objective – outcome mapping
and it was supported by all senior members.
4.3 Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad suggested that „Quality Policy‟ should be written at the
beginning of the Manual instead of page No 22nd.
4.4 He also enhanced the point that the first and foremost Quality Policy should be to
create good citizens and to make them aware towards their societal responsibility.
4.5 Important events like, programmes / workshops / Lectures on quality was tentatively
planned.
4.6 Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad and Dr. C. Srinagesh volunteered to conduct a few sessions for
the students as well as for the faculty to render various industry inputs regarding
quality.
4.7 Certain guidelines have been discussed for the formats and its functioning.
5. Other important matters regarding enhancing and sustaining quality were
discussed. –
5.1 Dr. C. Srinagesh said the objectives of IQAC should match the expectations of all
the stakeholders of the Institute and was supported by Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad.
5.2 General Secretary, VNRVJIET Dr D N Rao spoke on integrative learning and
advised IQAC to link up with the Institutional Spectral Pyramid;And was supported
by Ms Sandhya Kode.
5.3 Ms Sandhya Kode suggested to charter a data sheet of lead indicator and lag
indicator to mark a clear path for IQAC
5.4 Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad spoke on active learning methods. HOD EEE, HOD H&S and
HOD ECE supported with valuable inputs regarding innovative methods like
POGIL, CAREER VISION, and CERTIFICATE COURSES that are practised at the
Institute.
5.5 Students Mr. PS.K. Jayadev and Ms K.Savitha shared their positive experiences
about VNR Differentiators like, shadow Engineering, Mirror Engineering and
POGIL. They mentioned how these innovative efforts of the Institute are bringing in
quality teaching – learning measures.
5.6 Dr. Vijay Prasad advised on the FUNCTIONAL ASPECTS OF IQAC and
mentioned the importance of NAAC Accreditation and the importance of AQAR
Guidelines.
5.7 Director Dr. A. Subhananda Rao mentioned the significance of Research and
Consultancy to sustain quality and Mr J.V.R.S. Prasad rendered his helping hand to
provide guidance in this matter both for faculty and students.
5.8 Dean, Exam Branch explained the changes in evaluation and assessment that is
brought in after Autonomy status.
5.9 The meeting concluded with a positive note with Dr. C. Srinagesh appreciating
students of VNR VJIET as the most promising employees.
