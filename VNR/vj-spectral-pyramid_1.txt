VJ SPECTRAL PYRAMID, a differentiator practiced at VNR VJIET aims in
          bringing phenomenal changes in Academic, Administrative and Managerial
          processes, greatly benefiting all its stake holders. The face of this
          pyramid is “Exponent for Development of a Rounded Personality with
          Global Vision and Social Responsibility”. Executing this are the 17
          Professional Societies/Chapters and 15 Student Clubs of the institute,
          keeping the campus vibrant.
The co-curricular and extra-curricular expositions of these chapters
          help students grow beyond academic pursuits and appreciate the
          spectrum of life on a wider canvas through the curated development of
          artistic potential and professional competence. Thus, the activities
          provide the students ample opportunities to engage with experts and
          successful individuals and organizations in various domains, leading
          to their own personal as well as professional growth and paving a
          better-defined path for their lifelong learning.
TheStudent Council, a vital and integral part of the
          student community, participates in the institute development and also
          schedules the events calendar that contribute to Institute spirit and
          community welfare. This is a platform for the students to express
          their ideas, interests, and concerns about the institute.
VNR VJIET motivates and ensures students are an integral part of
          decision making in academic and administrative affairs. This will help
          students strengthen their perception in the decisions made. Student
          members are actively involved in the following Institute and
          Department-level committees:
Institute Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC):The
          nominated student member, involve in the scheduled periodical meetings
          of IQAC, with inputs supporting the implementation of qualitative
          initiatives.
Internal Complaints Committee:The student members
          voice the issues related to discrimination and bring it to the notice
          of Head of the Institute.
Library and Information Resource Committee:The
          concerns related to the availability of resources and renewals will be
          projected to see that the required books will be made available, and
          the concerns are addressed in time.
Sports Committee:Active participation of the student
          representatives, provides input for adequacy of facilities and
          scheduling of events without interference to the laid academic
          calendar and regulations.
Canteen Committee:Continuous monitoring of hygiene
          and food quality, projecting the peer’s concerns will be the
          responsibility of this student body.
Hostel Committee:A home away from home, the upkeep
          will be best maintained by its stakeholders. Students play a major
          role in ensuring quality food and hygiene, monitoring all facilities
          daily, and updating the faculty-incharge and the warden for the
          necessary for action.
Fest Organizing Committee:Organizing the annual
          events of the institute, “Convergence-The Technical Fest”,
          “Sintillashunz-The Cultural Fest”, “Ecficio- Entrepreneur Fest”, “Open
          House-The Project Expo”, and “Sports Fest”, without any hassle will be
          the responsibility of Student members of all Professional Societies
          and Student Clubs.
Disciplinary, Anti-Ragging, and Anti-Drug Committees:Students play crucial role in monitoring the campus activities for
          discipline and also participate in decision-making pertaining to
          disciplinary, ragging, and substance abuse incidents, if any, while
          also conducting awareness sessions for students about the and legal
          implications of ragging and substance abuse and ill effects thereof.
Editorial Boards for Vignana Vahini and Vignana Vartha:Institute magazines an assortment of various academic, co-curricular,
          and extra-curricular activities and achievements are put into print by
          the student editorial team, showcasing the talents of their peer and
          faculty on various platforms.
Class Review Committee (CRC):The faculty coordinator
          and 10 student representatives of the class, project conduct of
          classwork, delivery of lectures, and overall discipline, to Head of
          the department (HOD), for betterment action for class improvement.
Over and above, students are enthusiastic to join the social
          responsibility clubs such as NSS Units, VNRSF, N-Army, and Art of
          Living Club to participate in service activities like organizing blood
          donation camps, medical camps, health, hygiene, Yoga and improve
          awareness of rural health and sanitation, adult education, and
          environmental responsibility in the community.
The Institution has Adequate facilities for Cultural Activities,
            Yoga, Games (Indoor & Outdoor) and sports, (Gymnasium, Yoga Centre,
            Auditorium, etc.)
VNRVJIET's commitment to provide adequate facilities for cultural
          activities, yoga, games and sports demonstrates its recognition of the
          holistic development and well-roundedness of individuals. These
          facilities go beyond academics, allowing students and staff to engage
          in various activities that contribute to personal growth, exploration
          of interests, a healthy lifestyle, and the development of essential
          life skills. The Institute follows the principle of 'Healthy Mind in
          Healthy Body'.
Cultural ActivitiesThe institution recognizes the importance of cultural activities and
          provides adequate facilities to promote artistic expression,
          creativity, and the appreciation of different cultures. These
          activities play a significant role in enhancing the overall experience
          and well-being of students and staff.
Various student clubs are established, Stentorian- The English
          Literary club, Crescendo-The Music club, Creative arts club,
          Livewire-The dance club, Dramatics club, Nritya Tarang – The classical
          dance club, Scintillate- The Photography club, VJ Teatro- The short
          film club and VJ Sahithi Vanam- The Telugu literary club. Students
          actively engage in these clubs, practicing and participating in
          extracurricular activities. They also take part in national-level
          competitions, where they have achieved success by winning awards and
          prizes.
Student Activity Centre (SAC)is an exclusive
          facility with a built-up area of 525 square meters to host both
          professional societies and cultural clubs. This place is under the
          control of the students, allowing them to design and devise activities
          under their respective clubs and societies.
AuditoriumsThe institute has four air-conditioned auditoriums with seating
          capacity of 500, 300, 200 and 100 each. An open-air auditorium with a
          capacity of 10,000 is used to organize Induction program, Cultural
          Fest, Sports fest etc. The other three are used for showcasing the
          students’ professional and cultural talents.
GymnasiumAn exclusive gymnasium is available in an area of 169 Square meters at
          the Kode Venkatadri Chowdary Sports Complex (KVC Sports Complex) for
          students and faculty. This facility caters to maintain physical
          fitness and overall health of students and faculty. The Gym is
          equipped with 21 modern Independent Stations. Separate Gym facility is
          also available in both Boys and Girls hostels with the required number
          of stations.
Yoga CentreThe institute has a dedicated Yoga Centre in an area of about 100
          Square meters with a trained Yoga teacher to guide both students and
          faculty. Practicing yoga not only promotes physical well-being but
          also fosters personal transformation. The institution celebrates the
          International Day of Yoga, on June 21st every year. On that day the
          institute conducts a special event where all students and faculty
          actively participate. On the eve of the International Day of Yoga, the
          best performers are rewarded to encourage further engagement and
          participation. Exclusive Yoga centers are available in both Boys
          and Girls hostels. The institute also organizes courses through Art of
          Living foundation and Brahma Kumari’s on Yoga, meditation, and
          breathing techniques.
Games and Sports (Indoor Sports Facilities)The institute has state of the art sports complex named 'Kode
          Venkatadri Chowdary Sports Complex (KVC Sports Complex)' with a
          built-up area of 2,947 square meters, which accommodates indoor games
          and sports viz. Badminton, Basketball, Billiards &Snooker, Table
          Tennis, Carroms, Chess, Gym and Yoga Centre. The sports complex has
          the facility of changing rooms for both boys and girls separately. The
          locker facility is also available for students and staff.
Outdoor Sports FacilitiesThe institute has play fields over an area of 3.5 acres, which
          accommodates outdoor games and sports like Athletics, Basketball,
          Cricket, Football, Handball, Hockey, Kabaddi, Kho-Kho, Tennikoit,
          Tennis, Throwball, Volleyball.The institute organizes All India inter-university level tournaments
          in various events, including Athletics, Badminton, Basketball,
          Billiards, Cricket, Football, Handball, Hockey, Kabaddi, Kho-Kho,
          Table Tennis, Tennikoit, Tennis, Throwball and Volleyball for both
          boys and girls every year. Qualified trainers provide state-of-the-art
          training, and students have achieved notable success, winning medals,
          and accolades in National and International tournaments.
Medical CentreA separate medical Center is available with a full-time Medical
          Officer, Nurse, and adequate supply of medicines. The Centre ensures
          the students and staff have convenient access to healthcare services.
          The center is also equipped with an ambulance, oxygen concentrators
          and cylinders to serve the people with serious health conditions in
          emergencies. The institute has MOU's with Mamata Academy of Medical
          Sciences, Bachupally which is about 4Km from the institute and with
          Sri Sri Holistic Hospital, Nizampet about 8 Km from the institute. Any
          medical emergency requirements for both staff and students to use the
          services of these two hospitals.
Details of facilities in Institution for cultural activities,
              Yoga, Games (Indoor & Outdoor) and sports, Gymnasium, Yoga Centre,
              Auditorium
The institution spends approximately an amount of Rs.40 Lakhs per
              year on organizing various fests such as Sports, Cultural,
              Technical etc.
