ABOUT THE EVENT
"CONVERGENCE," the National Level Technical Symposium, has been redefining conventional
coursework since its inception in 1999. Over the years, it has evolved into the ultimate platform for tech
enthusiasts, fostering innovation and collaboration. Spanning two days, the event brings together students
from diverse disciplines, expanding their horizons through a dynamic lineup of activities. With a blend of
competitions, workshops, presentations, seminars, and guest lectures, each edition of CONVERGENCE
continues to inspire and challenge the next generation of innovators. Over the years, it has evolved into a
dynamic platform that fosters creativity, technical prowess, and interdisciplinary collaboration among
students. Beyond complementing academic coursework, CONVERGENCE serves as a launchpad for
aspiring engineers, empowering them to showcase their skills, exchange knowledge, and push the
boundaries of conventional thinking.
INAUGURAL
The National Annual Technical Symposium, “Convergence 2k25” was inaugurated at VNR Vignana
Jyothi Institute of Engineering and Technology (VNRVJIET) on 21st February 2025. Venkata Sai Kiran C,
Vice-President, Materials and Strategic Initiatives, Sky root Aerospace Pvt. Ltd. And Founder of AnanthaP
Research Pvt. Ltd., attended the inaugural as the Special Guests of the occasion.
Addressing the event, Dr. Venkata Sai Kiran Chakravadhanula, a renowned materials science
researcher and Skyroot Aerospace leader, emphasized the revolutionary impact of automation, materials
science, and digital manufacturing in defining the future of New India's TECHADE. Based on his rich
experience in academia and industry, he emphasized the need for sustainability, self-reliance, and innovation
as the main pillars for national development and international competitiveness.
Dr. Kiran provided reflections on his work in functional nanocomposites, high-performance alloys,
and electrochemical materials based on their contribution to aerospace and strategic advances. He also
touched upon the application of his work in electron microscopy and tomography to understand material
behaviors on the nanoscale.
Sri. Durga Prasad Kode, General Secretary of Vignana Jyothi, highlighted India's past leadership
across different areas and the country's present growth momentum in fields such as defense and aerospace.
He underlined that it is crucial to transcend silo mentality and develop a collaborative culture.
Prof. Padma Shayi, Dean-Student Progression, Prof. B. Chennakesava Rao, Director for
Advancement, and Prof. C.D. Naidu, Principal, urged the students to adopt new technologies and tap the
Institute's innovation ecosystem. They stated that the vision of the Institute goes beyond placements to
motivate students to be entrepreneurs and changemakers.
Prof. Padma Shayi also emphasized that the fests will provide students with an opportunity to express
new ideas with gamified events, competitions, workshops, panel discussions, and other social activities. The
event also witnessed the launch of the CONVERGENCE 2K25 souvenir, preceded by a captivating welcome
dance show by Nritya Tarang members of the classical dance club.
Sri K. Harishchandra Prasad, in his address emphasized the pace of innovation in automation, digital
finance, and industrial progress, emphasizing that current students possess a unique opportunity to lead the
pace of innovation. Relying on his extensive experience as a corporate leader and strategic business
development, he exhorted students to merge technology with business savvy in order to craft sustainable
solutions.
Dr. D.N. Rao, emphasized that the key to meaningful progress lies in transforming ideas into
impactful solutions. He encouraged students to break conventional barriers and actively engage in
technological research, urging them to think beyond the classroom and develop solutions that address
practical challenges. He motivated students to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth, reinforcing
that a strong foundation in innovation and critical thinking would pave the way for a brighter,
technologically advanced future.
To further amplify this spirit of ingenuity, CONVERGENCE 2k25 introduces Hardware and
Software Hackathons, Social Impact and Design Thinking Project Competitions, and Startup & Patent Idea
Competitions. These additions, along with staple events such as Technical Paper and Poster Presentations,
Technical Workshops, and other domain-specific technical challenges, reinforce the symposium’s
commitment to fostering a culture of research, entrepreneurship, and societal responsibility. With an
emphasis on sustainability and impactful technological solutions, the event not only highlights academic
excellence but also promotes meaningful discussions that address real-world challenges.
CONVERGENCE 2k25 promises to be a landmark event—one that inspires students to redefine the
future of technology while upholding the values of knowledge, creativity, and social consciousness.

VALEDICTORY
The grand finale of Convergence 2K25, VNR Vignana Jyothi Institute of Engineering and Technology
(VNRVJIET) esteemed technical fest, ended with a thought-provoking Valedictory Ceremony marked by the
presence of honored dignitaries, faculty, students, and industry leaders. The festival highlighted
technological leaps, student inventions, and knowledge-sharing sessions that were the hallmark of the fest in
the last few days. The valedictory session was addressed by Mr. Bala Prasad Peddigari, Principal
Consultant and Chief Innovation Officer, Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), a veteran technologist with
more than 26 years of experience. He gave a keynote address on "Futurenomics – The Business of Emerging
Technologies" and gave a futuristic vision on how AI, quantum computing, blockchain, and automation are
transforming industries and redefining the future of work. His thoughts on technology integration, co-
innovation, and digital transformation motivated young minds to accept the changing technological
environment.
The fest saw more than 2825 students participate, exhibiting their technical skills through project
exhibitions, workshops, and technical competitions. These events encouraged students to learn through
hands-on experience, enhancing creativity and problem-solving skills. Cultural and networking sessions
helped in interaction among academia, industry professionals, and aspiring technologists. The Prize
Distribution Ceremony was a highlight of the event, attended by Principal Dr. C. D. Naidu, Director -
Advancement and Dean - Administration Dr. B. Chennakesava Rao and Dr. Padma Shayi Dean Student
Progression. They awarded prizes to the winners of different competitions and honored exceptional
contributions made by students and faculty.
The success of Convergence 2K25 reiterated VNRVJIET's mission of developing technological
excellence and making students ready for future challenges. The presence of distinguished personalities such
as Mr. Bala Prasad Peddigari contributed significantly to the event, providing students with valuable
exposure to the latest trends in technology and business. With the massive enthusiasm and interest seen this
year, the organizing committee hopes to have an even more powerful and transformative edition of
Convergence in the years to come. The proceedings were finally ended with a vote of thanks to thank
everyone participating, sponsoring, faculty, and volunteers for contributing to making Convergence 2K25 an
overwhelming success.

HACKATHON
About the event :- The Hackathon at Convergence was a dynamic and intellectually stimulating
event that challenged participants to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems. As a key
highlight of the technical fest, it provided a platform for students to showcase their technical skills,
creativity, and problem-solving abilities in a competitive yet collaborative environment.
The competition featured real-world problem statements, allowing teams to work on diverse
technological challenges to develop software, hardware and hybrid solutions. Participants were
encouraged to develop prototypes and applications in 24 hours, testing their ability to think
critically, work under pressure, and implement effective solutions.
The Hackathon was structured into two rounds: Round 1 required teams to submit their proposed
solutions in PPT format, outlining their approach and feasibility. Shortlisted teams advanced to
Round 2, a rigorous 24-hour Hackathon held on campus, where they transformed their ideas into
working prototypes.
Throughout the event, participants benefited from mentoring sessions, where industry experts and
faculty members provided guidance and technical support. Regular checkpoints ensured teams
stayed on track, refining their solutions as they progressed. The final round featured intense
presentations, where a jury panel evaluated the projects based on innovation, technical
implementation, feasibility, and overall impact.
The Hackathon at Convergence was an engaging and impactful experience, fostering innovation,
teamwork, and technical excellence. It provided students with invaluable exposure to real-world
problem-solving while reinforcing the importance of collaboration and creativity in the ever-
evolving tech industry.

ROBOQUEST – Unleash Your Robotic Potential
About the Event
ROBOQUEST – Unleash Your Robotic Potential – was an engaging and intellectually stimulating two-day event
conducted as part of CONVERGENCE 2K25, the national-level technical symposium at VNR VJIET. This event
was designed to challenge students in the domain of robotic programming and simulation, providing them with an
opportunity to gain hands-on experience in cutting-edge robotics technology. With automation becoming an
integral part of modern industries, ROBOQUEST aimed to equip students with practical skills in robotic
simulation and motion control using Fanuc Roboguide—one of the most widely used robotic programming
platforms in the manufacturing and automation sectors.
The event structure was meticulously planned to balance both learning and competition, ensuring that every
participant walked away with enhanced knowledge, practical exposure, and an opportunity to showcase their
problem-solving and programming capabilities.
Number of Participants: 15
Venue: D418
Date: 21/02/2025 - 22/02/2025
Timings: 9:00 am - 3:00 pm
Event Structure
Day 1 – Hands-on Training Session
The first day was dedicated to learning and skill development, where participants were introduced to Fanuc
Roboguide software by experienced instructors. This session covered essential topics such as:
• Introduction to robotic simulation and automation
• Understanding robot motion control and path planning
• Fundamentals of Fanuc Roboguide software and its interface
• Programming industrial robots to perform specific tasks
• Hands-on exercises to apply learning in a simulated environment
Additionally, participants were trained in programming for path tracing of a square in Roboguide. This exercise
enabled them to grasp fundamental motion control techniques. Furthermore, they were guided through the
simulation of pick-and-place operations, enhancing their understanding of industrial automation processes.
The goal of Day 1 was to ensure that all participants, regardless of their prior knowledge, could grasp the
fundamental concepts of robotic programming. The interactive nature of the session allowed students to engage
with mentors, clear doubts, and gain practical exposure to industry-relevant automation techniques.
Day 2 – The ROBOQUEST Competition
The second day marked the high-stakes competition, where participants had to apply their newly acquired skills
to solve real-world robotic challenges. The competition was designed to test their:
• Programming efficiency – Writing optimized and functional robot control code
• Problem-solving skills – Analyzing challenges and designing effective solutions
• Time management and execution – Completing assigned tasks within given constraints
Participants were given specific problem statements, requiring them to program robotic arms to perform predefined
tasks. The tasks included:
• Path tracing of a hexagonal shape, testing precision in robotic motion control
• Simulation of pick-and-place operations, assessing accuracy and execution efficiency
Each participant was evaluated based on efficiency, accuracy, and innovation in their solutions. The competition
required individuals to write and implement robotic programming code independently. The competitive
atmosphere pushed them to think critically, debug efficiently, and refine their approach under time constraints,
simulating industry-like problem-solving scenarios.

DIKSUCHI
Date: 21-02-2025 and 22-02-2025
Venue: Convergence Stall
No. Of Attendees: 150
Diksuchi means a compass that shows the right directions for traveling. In the same way, the
event aimed to showcase the opportunities available for the students that would boost their
performance. DIKSUCHI was one of the events organized by the Team Student Force in the
convergence of 2025.
As part of our Diksuchi event, we structured our explanation into four key topics, each
designed to provide valuable insights and opportunities for students.
1. Government Job Opportunities
In the first phase of our discussion, we highlighted
the various government job opportunities available
after completing both the 10th grade and a B.Tech
degree. We explained how eligibility criteria and
requirements vary depending on the type of job
and the level of qualification. This segment aimed to
create awareness about stable and rewarding career
options in the public sector, encouraging students to
explore pathways that align with their skills and
aspirations.
2. Internship Opportunities
In the second topic, we emphasized the significance of internships and their role in bridging
the gap between academic learning and real-world experience. Internships provide students,
recent graduates, and professionals seeking hands-on experience with opportunities to apply
their theoretical knowledge in practical work environments. We explained how internships
can vary in duration, from a few weeks to several months, and may be paid or unpaid,
depending on the company or organization offering them. By gaining industry exposure,
interns develop crucial skills, build professional networks, and enhance their employability.
3. Fellowship Programs
The third topic focused on raising awareness about various fellowship programs and their
benefits. Fellowships are specialized programs—often funded by universities, organizations,
foundations, or governments—that provide financial support, mentorship, and resources to
individuals pursuing advanced studies, research, or professional development in a specific
field. We aimed to inspire students to explore these opportunities, which can open doors to
academic excellence, career advancement, and global networking.
4. Freelancing and Its Importance
In the final topic, we introduced the concept of
freelancing and its growing significance in today’s
dynamic job market. Freelancing allows individuals
to work independently, offering their skills and
expertise to clients on a project basis rather than
being tied to a single employer. Freelancers have the
flexibility to choose their workload, work remotely,
and engage with multiple clients across various
industries. We highlighted how freelancing
empowers individuals to build their own careers,
develop entrepreneurial skills, and earn a sustainable
income while enjoying work life balance.
Through these four topics, we aimed to provide students with valuable knowledge and
practical insights, equipping them with the tools to make informed career decisions and
explore diverse professional avenues.

SPARSHA
Date: 21-02-2025 and 22-02-2025
Venue: Convergence Stall
No. Of Attendees: 150
Sparsha was an engaging event held as part of SAARADHI during Convergence
2025, aimed at fostering empathy among students for physically disabled individuals.
The event provided participants with an opportunity to experience, in a small but
impactful way, the challenges faced by those with disabilities.
The primary activity involved blindfolding participants and challenging them to
complete tasks that would normally be easy to perform without any impairments. By
doing so, students were given a glimpse into the struggles of people who navigate life
without one of their senses. The event was well-received, with students gaining a
greater understanding of the difficulties faced by those with visual impairments and
developing a sense of empathy and appreciation for their resilience.
Games Conducted Under Sparsha
One of the most popular games during Sparsha was DEAF DUM BLIND, a fun and
thought-provoking activity that required students to participate in various challenges
while blindfolded. In this game, two players would take turns selecting two
combinations of slips. Each combination corresponded to a different game. Once the
combinations were selected, the players engaged in the following games:
❖ Deaf & Blind → Pictionary: One player would attempt to draw something
while being blindfolded, and the other player would try to guess what it was.
This tested participants’ abilities to communicate without sight.
❖ Deaf & Dum → Dumb Charades: In this round, players had to communicate
and act out phrases or words without speaking, simulating the challenge of
communication faced by people with hearing or speech impairments.
❖ Dum & Blind → Source to Destination: In this task, participants,
blindfolded, had to navigate from a starting point to a destination, relying on
their other senses and a partner’s guidance. This highlighted the difficulty of
performing tasks without sight, fostering empathy for the visually impaired.

About the Event:
CADATHON was an engaging and competitive event that challenged participants to showcase their CAD skills in both
product design and floor planning. The competition included two major segments: Product Design using Fusion 360 and
Floor Design using AutoCAD. The event was structured in two levels, with eliminations after the first level to ensure only
the most skilled participants advanced.
The product design challenge was conducted using Fusion 360. In Level 1, participants were provided with a 3D model,
which they had to replicate accurately using Fusion 360. This round tested their modeling proficiency and attention to detail.
In Level 2, shortlisted participants were given a design statement provided by BANDIWALA, a startup incubated at VJ
HUB. The design statement required innovative problem-solving and creative thinking, aligning with real-world product
development challenges.
The floor design competition was conducted using AutoCAD and spanned two days. On Day 1, Level 1 participants were
given a basic floor plan to draft. This round tested their fundamental drafting skills and understanding of architectural
layouts. On Day 2, the selected participants from Level 1 moved on to designing an elevation plan, requiring them to
showcase their ability to visualize and translate 2D layouts into structured elevations.
After the first level in both segments, a screening process was conducted to ensure that only the best performers progressed
to the second round. This allowed for a more competitive and high-quality final stage, where participants had to demonstrate
advanced skills and design thinking.
CADATHON successfully provided students with an opportunity to enhance and demonstrate their CAD proficiency in
both product and architectural design. The involvement of BANDIWALA as a jury added an industry perspective, making
the event more impactful. The competition not only tested technical abilities but also encouraged problem-solving,
creativity, and practical application of CAD software in real-world scenarios.

FIGMA UI/UX
About the Workshop :
The Figma UI/UX Workshop, conducted as part of Convergence 2k25 at VNRVJIET, was an
insightful session that introduced participants to UI/UX design fundamentals. Led by Sri Nikhil C,
a freelance UI/UX designer, the workshop provided a deep dive into the significance of design,
career opportunities in UI/UX, and the Product Development Life Cycle. The session covered
essential aspects such as understanding UI & UX, the importance of design, domains of UX, and
the various stages of the design process. Participants explored how effective UI/UX enhances user
satisfaction and the role of designers in shaping digital experiences.
The workshop also featured interactive activities, where participants conducted peer interviews to
gather insights about a campus app, identifying user goals and frustrations with the current
system. This was followed by an engaging user persona creation exercise, helping attendees
understand the importance of designing for specific user needs. Additionally, the session
introduced wireframing concepts, covering different types of wireframes and their role in product
design. Participants then applied what they had learned in a hands-on wireframing activity,
sketching app layouts to visualize their ideas. With Figma as the primary tool, the session
equipped attendees with practical skills to kickstart their UI/UX journey.
The workshop was an enriching experience, fostering creativity, user-centered thinking, and a
deeper appreciation for design principles in digital product development.

GEARS & GREASE
The Gears & Grease workshop was an exhilarating experience for all mechanical enthusiasts,
offering a unique opportunity to assemble and disassemble an engine while gaining in-depth
knowledge of its components and functionality. The event provided a perfect platform for
participants to get hands-on with real-world engineering, bridging the gap between theoretical
learning and practical application.
The session began with a step-by-step disassembly of the engine, where we carefully dismantled
each component while explaining its function and significance in the overall system. From the
cylinder block to the crankshaft, camshaft, pistons, valves, and spark plugs, every part was
thoroughly examined. Participants got a chance to understand the intricate design and precision
required in assembling an engine.
Once the engine was disassembled, we delved into the working principles behind it. We discussed
the four-stroke cycle (intake, compression, power, and exhaust), how fuel combustion generates
power, and the importance of systems like lubrication, cooling, and fuel injection. Each concept
was explained with real-life examples to make it easier to grasp.
After exploring the internals, it was time to put everything back together! Participants actively
engaged in the reassembly process, ensuring that every component was correctly positioned and
aligned. This hands-on task not only reinforced their understanding of engine mechanics but
also honed their practical skills.
The workshop was highly interactive, with participants asking questions, sharing insights, and
discussing various engineering concepts. The hands-on nature of the workshop allowed everyone
to experience the thrill of bringing an engine to life, making it an unforgettable learning
experience.
The workshop successfully blended theory with practical application, ensuring that participants
walked away with enhanced technical skills and a stronger foundation in automotive engineering.

IDEA TO IPO
About the Workshop :-
As a crucial element of Convergence 2k25, We team IUCEE (Indo-Universal Collaboration for
Engineering Education) organised workshop on Idea to IPO. Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are
legal protections granted to individuals or organizations for their creations and inventions. They
encourage innovation by giving creators exclusive rights to their work for a certain period
Intellectual property rights (IPR) are crucial for protecting your creations and ensuring you have
exclusive rights to your innovations. Here’s a quick summary of the types of IPR you mentioned:
Research and Validate: Ensure your idea is original. Conduct thorough research to see if similar
ideas exist and understand the existing competition.
Documentation: Keep detailed records of your idea's development. Document every step, from
conception to implementation. This documentation will be crucial when filing for IPR.
Choose the Right Type of IPR: Depending on your idea, you might need a patent, trademark,
copyright, or trade secret protection.
❖ Patents are for inventions and grant exclusive rights to make, use, or sell the invention for a
certain period.
❖ Trademarks protect brand names, slogans, logos, and symbols.
❖ Copyrights are for original works of authorship, including literary, musical, and artistic works.
❖ Trade secrets protect confidential business information that provides a competitive edge.
Patent Search: If you're considering a patent, perform a patent search to ensure no existing patents
overlap with your invention. This can save you time and money before you proceed with the
application.
Consult an IPR Attorney: Consulting with an Intellectual Property Rights attorney can provide
valuable guidance and ensure you take the right steps to protect your idea.
File Your Application: Depending on the type of protection you need, file your application with
the appropriate authority. For patents and trademarks, this would typically be your country's patent
and trademark office.
Monitor and Enforce: Once your IPR is granted, regularly monitor for any infringements and be
prepared to enforce your rights if necessary.
They provide creators and inventors with legal protections, ensuring they can benefit from their
work. This protection encourages more investment in research and development, leading to
economic growth and technological advancements. IPR helps businesses differentiate their products
and services, building brand reputation and consumer trust. It also promotes knowledge sharing, as
inventors are more willing to disclose their innovations, knowing they have legal safeguards.

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are essential tools that provide creators and inventors with legal

sustainable transportation. It provides a hands-on understanding of electric powertrains, focusing
on components such as motors, controllers, and battery systems, while also addressing the critical
role of power electronics in energy conversion and efficiency. Participants explore various battery
chemistries, thermal management techniques, and the importance of an optimized Battery
Management System (BMS) to enhance battery lifespan and safety. The workshop also sheds light
on charging infrastructure, discussing the differences between AC and DC charging, the challenges
of fast-charging networks, and emerging wireless charging solutions. Beyond the technical aspects,
it examines the broader industry landscape, including government incentives, environmental
impacts, and the latest innovations in solid-state batteries, hydrogen fuel cells, and autonomous EV
technology. Additionally, practical sessions on vehicle simulation tools, IoT integration, and AI-
driven diagnostics prepare attendees for real-world applications, making the workshop an
invaluable learning experience for engineers, researchers, and industry professionals looking to
contribute to the future of electric mobility.

KAKSYA SASTRA WORKSHOP ON ROCKETRY & ROCKET
BUILDING
Details of the Event:
Speaker Bio: Natarajan Rajavel is the founder and CEO of Rocket Factory
India, operated under Rocketry Tamil. He belongs to Chidambaram and is
involved in producing model rockets and promoting awareness about rocketry
through practical workshops and informative articles. His blog encompasses
space research, ISRO and SpaceX developments, simplifying complex space
science into simple language for all. He holds a physics degree from Government
Arts College, C. Mutlur, and utilizes his knowledge and passion for rocketry to
create awareness and inspire young minds who are interested in space.
Topics: This workshop covered the fundamental principles of rocketry,
including rocket physics, orbits, and satellites. Participants explored different
types of rockets, understood what a sounding rocket is, and learned how to
build one. The session included a live demonstration, offering hands-on
experience in model rocketry.
● Introduction to space and rocketry
● Basics of rocket design and components
● Understanding orbits and satellite systems
● Types of rockets and their applications
● What is a sounding rocket & how to build one
● Model rocketry demonstration

Conclusion of the Event:
In conclusion, the Rocketry and Rocket building workshop was a fun, interesting
and inspiring dive into the world of rockets. We learned about the history of
rocket science in India, how they work, and we learn’t about the different types
of rockets that are used by Private and government space agencies around the
world with a special focus on ISRO(Indian Space Research Organisation). The
model rocket demonstration made things even more exciting, and the Q&A
session cleared up doubts while sparking curiosity. Overall, the event was a huge
success, encouraging students to delve deeper into the topic of rocketry and
space exploration. This session has indeed ignited a fire among young engineers
and space enthusiasts to continue learning and innovating in this fascinating field.
