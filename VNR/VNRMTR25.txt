1.0 PREAMBLE:
1.1 VNR Vignana Jyothi Institute of Engineering and Technology (VNRVJIET) is committed
to achieving academic excellence through innovative teaching-learning processes,
continuous curriculum enhancement and robust evaluation mechanisms.
1.2 In line with the guidelines of the University Grants Commission (UGC), All India Council
for Technical Education (AICTE) and Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University
Hyderabad (JNTUH), the Institute introduced the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS)
for all Undergraduate and Postgraduate Programmes from the academic year 2015-
2016 and continues its implementation under the Academic Regulations stipulated
herein.
1.3 The implementation of the CBCS at VNRVJIET is intended to align with national
initiatives for standardization of higher education and to enhance student flexibility,
academic mobility, interdisciplinary learning and skill development.
1.4 In consonance with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, the Institute has
incorporated several academic reforms and curricular initiatives into these Academic
Regulations and Curriculum.
1.5 These initiatives are designed to foster holistic development, promote multidisciplinary
and interdisciplinary learning and prepare graduates to meet global, ethical and
societal challenges, while upholding the Institute’s commitment to academic quality
and excellence.
2.0 SHORT TITLE, APPLICATION AND COMMENCEMENT:
2.1 These Regulations shall be called the “VNRVJIET M.Tech. Choice Based Credit System
(CBCS) Academic Regulations, 2025” and may be referred to as the “VNRVJIET M.Tech.
CBCS R25 Regulations”.
2.2 These Regulations shall apply to all M.Tech. Regular (Full-Time) Postgraduate
Programmes offered by the Institute.
2.3 These Regulations shall come into force for all student batches admitted from the
academic year 2025-2026 onwards.

2.4 These Regulations are comprehensive and include, inter alia, provisions relating to the
definitions of key terms, the semester system, the credit system, the grading system,
Programme Structure, the Scheme of Examinations and Evaluation procedures.
2.5 The Institute may, from time to time, revise, amend, or update these Regulations, the
scheme of examinations, and the syllabi, based on the recommendations of the
respective Boards of Studies (BoS), subject to the approval of the Academic Council.
2.6 Any such revisions, amendments, or updates shall take effect from the date specified
and shall be applicable to such batches of students, including those already
undergoing the programme, as approved by the Academic Council.
3.0 CHOICE BASED CREDIT SYSTEM:
3.1 The Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) is an academic framework that enables a
student to qualify for the award of a degree by earning the prescribed number of
credits as specified in the Programme Curriculum. The credits earned represent the
academic workload and learning effort associated with a course.
3.2 Each course is assigned a fixed number of credits based on its depth, breadth and
intended learning outcomes. Student performance in each course is evaluated through
a grading system and the grade points obtained reflect the level of proficiency
achieved.
3.3 The CBCS offers the following features and advantages:
i. Flexibility in course selection: Students may choose elective courses from a broad
spectrum offered by various departments.
ii. Opportunities for additional learning: Students may register for courses beyond
the minimum prescribed requirements to enhance their knowledge and skills.
iii. Interdisciplinary learning: Students may adopt an interdisciplinary approach by
selecting courses across different domains.
iv. Academic mobility: Credits earned from other departments or recognized higher
education institutions in India may be transferred, subject to approval from the
Academic Council and University norms.
v. Utilization of faculty expertise: Students can benefit from specialized courses
offered by faculty members with domain expertise.
3.4 The CBCS permits flexibility in completing the programme of study within the
maximum duration prescribed under these Regulations.

9.0 M.TECH. PROGRAMME STRUCTURE:
9.1 The structure of the M.Tech. Degree Programmes offered at VNRVJIET shall be in
accordance with the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) and the Credit Based Semester
System (CBSS), as defined by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and aligned with
the Model Curriculum prescribed by the All India Council for Technical Education
(AICTE) and Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Hyderabad (JNTUH).
9.2 The total credits prescribed for M.Tech. Degree Programme and for the award of the
M.Tech. Degree shall be 68.
9.3 Semester Scheme:
9.3.1 The M.Tech. Degree Programmes shall follow the semester scheme with the following
provisions:
9.3.1.1 Each academic year shall consist of two (2) semesters, namely Odd and Even semesters,
sequentially numbered from I to IV Semester.
9.3.1.2 Each semester shall comprise seventeen (17) weeks, excluding the Sessional
Examinations and Semester End Examinations.
9.3.1.3 Each semester shall comprise Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) and Semester End
Examination (SEE) components, as prescribed in these Regulations.
10.0 DURATION OF THE ACADEMIC PROGRAMMES:
10.1 The normal duration of the M.Tech. Degree Programme shall be two (2) academic years
for students admitted to the I Year of the programme.
10.2 The maximum permissible duration for completion of the programme shall be twice
the normal duration, i.e., four (4) academic years for students admitted to the I Year.

12.0 COURSE REGISTRATION:
12.1 Course Registration Process:
12.1.1 A Faculty Advisor/Counselor shall be assigned to each specialization, who will advise
the students on the Post Graduate Programme, its Course Structure and Curriculum,
Choices/Options for Courses, based on their competence, progress, prerequisites and
interest.
12.1.2 Students shall register online for the prescribed courses of the semester through the
Campus Automation Management System (CAMS) within the dates specified in the
Academic Calendar, after obtaining approval from the Faculty Advisor/Mentor and the
Head of the Department.
12.1.3 Course registration shall be considered valid only upon approval in the online system,
fulfilment of prerequisites (if any) and payment of prescribed tuition fees and all dues,
including Institute and hostel dues.
12.1.4 In case of multiple submissions or erroneous entries during online registration, only
the earliest valid submission recorded in the system shall be treated as final.
12.1.5 Course choices exercised during online registration are final and cannot be changed
thereafter.
12.1.6 However, if a course listed for offering in that semester could not be offered due to
unavoidable circumstances, the Head of the Department may permit suitable
alternative course options and shall notify the students through a time-bound schedule
before commencement of classwork.
12.1.7 No course registration shall be permitted beyond the notified schedule.
12.1.8 In absentia or proxy registration shall not be permitted. Each student shall personally
complete course registration using their own credentials.
12.2 Open Elective Courses:
12.2.1 Students shall register for one Open Elective (OE) in III Semester from the list of
electives offered by departments other than their parent department, as per the
approved Programme Structure.
12.2.2 Students shall not register for an Open Elective offered by their parent department or
that is equivalent in content to any course prescribed in their Programme Curriculum.
12.2.3 Students shall not register for an Open Elective that has already been
completed/credited earlier, or that is equivalent to any course to be studied in
subsequent semesters.
12.3 Professional Elective Courses:
12.3.1 Students shall register for five Professional Elective (PE) courses (PE-I to PE-V) from the
baskets specified in the Programme Structure.
12.3.2 Students may choose Professional Electives from within their discipline or opt for
interdisciplinary Professional Electives offered by other departments, subject to
fulfillment of prerequisites or waiver approved by the Dean of Academics.

13.0 ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS AND DETENTION POLICY:
13.1 Attendance shall be calculated separately for each course. The minimum required
attendance in each Theory Course (including Audit Courses) inclusive of attendance for
Sessional Examinations, and Laboratory Course, shall be 75%.
13.2 A student shall be eligible to appear for the Semester End Examinations (SEE) only if
he/she secures a minimum of 75% attendance in each of the registered courses,
including Audit Courses, of the semester.
13.3 A student's Technical Seminar, Industry Engagement and Mini-Project shall be eligible
for evaluation, only if the student secures a minimum of 75% attendance in the
respective classes during that semester.
13.4 Condonation of Attendance: A shortage of attendance of up to 10% (i.e., between 65%
and 75%) in each course (Theory/Laboratory/Technical Seminar/Mini-Project, etc.) of a
semester may be condoned by the Institute Academic Committee on genuine and valid
grounds, subject to payment of the prescribed condonation fee as notified from time
to time.
13.5 Attendance credit equivalent to two lecture periods per theory course shall be awarded
to a student for appearing in the Sessional Examination of that course.
13.6 Attendance below 65% in any course shall not be condoned under any circumstances.
13.7 A student who has already fulfilled the attendance requirement of a semester shall not
be eligible for re-admission to the same semester.

14.0 MINIMUM ACADEMIC REQUIREMENTS:
14.1 In addition to the attendance requirements specified in Clause 13.0 of these
Regulations, a student shall satisfy the following minimum academic requirements for
the award of the M.Tech. Degree.
14.2 Theory and Practical Courses:
14.2.1 A student shall be deemed to have satisfied the minimum academic requirements and
15
earned the credits allotted to a Theory or Practical Course upon securing all of the
following:
a) A minimum of 40% marks in the Semester End Examination (SEE) of the course (i.e.,
24 marks out of 60)
b) A minimum of 50% marks in the aggregate of CIE and SEE taken together (i.e., 50
marks out of 100) and
c) A minimum of ‘B’ Grade or above in the course.
14.3 Technical Seminar, Industry Engagement and Mini-Project Courses:
14.3.1 A student shall be deemed to have satisfied the minimum academic requirements and
earned credits allotted to Technical Seminar, Industry Engagement and Mini-Project
Courses upon securing a minimum of 50% of the total marks (i.e., 50 marks out of 100
marks).
14.3.2 A student shall be treated as failed, if the student:
a) Does not submit the prescribed report on Technical Seminar, Industry Engagement,
and Mini-Project, or
b) Does not make the presentation before the Evaluation Committee as per the
notified schedule, or
c) Remains absent during the scheduled Project evaluation, or
d) Secures less than 50% marks (i.e., 50 marks out of 100 marks) in the overall
evaluation.
14.3.3 A failed student shall reappear for the above evaluation when the notification for the
supplementary examination is issued.
14.4 Credit Requirements for the Award of Degree:
14.4.1 To successfully complete the M.Tech. Degree Programme, a student shall:
a) Register for courses covering a total of 68 credits, as specified in the approved
course structure of the chosen specialization
b) Fulfill all the prescribed attendance and academic requirements
c) Earn all 68 credits by securing a minimum of ‘B’ Grade or above in each course and
d) Pass all the prescribed Audit Courses.
14.4.2 The Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) shall be computed and printed on the marks
memo only if the student passes all the courses registered in that semester and secures
a minimum of ‘B’ Grade or above in each course.
14.4.3 The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) shall be calculated only when the student
passes all the courses offered in all the semesters of the M.Tech. Programme.
14.4.4 Only the Letter Grades obtained in courses covering the prescribed 68 credits shall be
considered for the calculation of the final CGPA, which shall be indicated in the
Consolidated Grade Memo.
14.5 Detention Due to Shortage of Attendance:
14.5.1 A student detained in any course(s) (Theory/Laboratory/Technical Seminar/Mini-
Project, etc.) in a semester due to shortage of attendance shall not be eligible to appear
for the Semester End Examination(s) of such course(s).
14.5.2 Such a student shall be permitted to re-register for the detained course(s) in the
subsequent semester(s), as and when the course(s) are next offered, by paying the
prescribed fee per course. The Academic Regulations applicable to the batch into which
the student is re-registered shall apply.
14.5.3 In all cases of course re-registration, the student shall secure a fresh set of internal
marks and Semester End Examination marks, which shall be considered for SGPA and
16
CGPA calculations.
14.5.4 A student who does not meet the minimum required attendance in a semester as per
the promotion policy defined in 13.8, shall be detained in that semester and the
registration for that semester shall stand cancelled, including all academic credentials
such as internal marks. Such a student shall not be promoted to the next semester and
shall seek re-admission to the same semester when it is offered in a subsequent
academic year. The Academic Regulations in force at the time of re-admission shall be
applicable.
14.5.5 A student who has not paid the prescribed tuition fee or has any outstanding dues to
the Institute shall not be permitted to appear for the Semester End Examinations.
14.5.6 If the prescribed Professional/Open Electives are not offered during the semester of
re-admission, the student shall register for approved alternative electives from the
same category, as notified by the Department.
14.5.7 Students under disciplinary suspension shall not be permitted to appear for the
Semester End Examinations.
14.6 Supplementary Examinations:
14.6.1 A student who is eligible to appear for the Semester End Examination of a course but
remains absent or fails (fails to secure ‘B’ Grade or above) shall be permitted to
reappear for that course in the supplementary Semester End Examination, as and when
conducted.
14.6.2 In such cases, the CIE marks obtained earlier shall be carried over and added to the
marks secured in the supplementary Semester End Examination for evaluating the
performance in that course.
14.7 Maximum Duration to Earn Credits:
14.7.1 A student shall earn the prescribed 68 credits within a maximum period of four (4)
academic years from the date of admission to I Semester (I Year I Semester); failing
which, the student shall forfeit the seat in the M.Tech. Programme and the admission
shall stand cancelled.

15.0 DISTRIBUTION OF MARKS AND EVALUATION:
15.1 General:
15.1.1 The performance of a student in every registered course, including Theory,
Practical/Laboratory, Skill Development Courses, Technical Seminar, Industry
Engagement, and Mini-Project shall be evaluated for a total of 100 marks.
15.1.2 For all courses, except those otherwise specified, the evaluation pattern shall consist

(a) Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE): 40 Marks
(b) Semester End Examination (SEE): 60 Marks
15.2 Theory Courses:
15.2.1 Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE):
15.2.1.1 Each Theory course shall carry forty (40) marks for Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE).
15.2.1.2 The CIE shall consist of:
• Sessional Examinations (SE): 30 marks
• Class Assessment (CA): 5 marks
• Experiential Learning Assessment (ELA): 5 marks
15.2.1.3 Two Sessional Examinations, designated as SE-I and SE-II, each of two (2) hours
duration and carrying thirty (30) marks, shall be conducted as per the Academic
Calendar.
15.2.1.4 The syllabus coverage for SE-I shall include Unit I, Unit II and the first half of Unit III,
whereas for SE-II shall include the second half of Unit III, Unit IV and Unit V.
15.2.1.5 The question paper pattern for Sessional Examinations shall be as follows:
i) Part-A: 5Q x 2M = 10M
• Five (5) compulsory short-answer questions of 2 marks each.
• Two questions each from Unit-I and Unit-II and one question from the first half of
Unit-III in First Sessional Examination (SE-I)
• One question from the second half of Unit-III and 2 questions each from Unit-IV
and Unit-V in Second Sessional Examination (SE-II)
ii) Part-B: 20M
• Six descriptive questions, i.e., two questions from each unit, with internal choice i.e.,
‘either’ ‘or’ choice.
• The student shall answer one question from each unit.
• A maximum of two sub-divisions i.e. (a) and/or (b) shall be permitted in a question.
a) Sessional Examination-I (SE-I):
• Unit I: Q.1 OR Q.2 8 marks
• Unit II: Q.3 OR Q.4 8 marks
• Unit III: Q.5 OR Q.6 4 marks
b) Sessional Examination-II (SE-II):
• Unit III: Q.1 OR Q.2 4 marks
• Unit IV: Q.3 OR Q.4 8 marks
• Unit V: Q.5 OR Q.6 8 marks
18
iii) The average of SE-I and SE-II marks shall constitute the final Sessional Examination
score for 30 marks.
15.2.1.6 Class Assessment (CA) shall be conducted twice during the semester as per the
Academic Calendar using Alternative Assessment Tools (AAT), such as assignments,
quizzes, online tests, or objective examinations. The average of the two assessments
shall constitute five (5) marks for CA.
15.2.1.7 Experiential Learning Assessment (ELA) shall be conducted twice during the semester
and may include AAT such as course projects, viva-voce, poster presentations, case
studies, open-ended problems, simulations, or computational assignments. The
average of the two ELAs shall constitute five (5) marks for ELA.
15.2.1.8 The total CIE marks i.e., forty (40 marks) for Theory Courses shall be the sum of:
• Average of the Sessional Examinations
• Average of the Class Assessment and
• Average of the Experiential Learning Assessment marks
15.2.2 Semester End Examination (SEE):
15.2.2.1 The Semester End Examination (SEE) for Theory Courses shall be conducted for a
duration of three (3) hours at the end of the semester and shall carry sixty (60) marks.
15.2.2.2 The SEE question paper shall consist of two parts.
i) Part-A: 5Q x 2M=10M
• Five (5) compulsory short-answer questions, one from each unit of the syllabus
carrying 2 marks each.
ii) Part-B: 5Q x 10M=50M
• Part-B shall carry fifty (50) marks comprising ten (10) descriptive questions, two (2)
from each unit with internal choice, i.e., ‘either’ ‘or’ choice.
• The student shall answer one question from each unit.
• There shall be a maximum of two sub-divisions (a) and/or (b) in any question.
15.2.2.3 All units shall carry equal weightage and the SEE shall cover the entire syllabus
prescribed for the course.

15.4 Technical Seminar:
15.4.1 Each student shall deliver a Technical Seminar during the I Semester (I Year I Semester)

15.4.2 Under the supervision of a faculty member, the student shall select a technical topic of

Academics.
15.4.3 The evaluation of the Technical Seminar shall be carried out by the Seminar Review

five percent (25%) and shall not include AI-generated content, in accordance with the

after two re-submissions, he/she shall be deemed to have failed in the Technical
Seminar and shall be required to re-register for it in the subsequent semester.
15.4.9 To be declared successful in the Technical Seminar, the student shall secure a minimum
of fifty percent (50%) of the total marks in the combined evaluation of CIE and SEE.

15.7 M.Tech. Dissertation Work:
15.7.1 General:
15.7.1.1 Every student shall be required to carry out Dissertation Work, aimed at enhancing
analytical ability, research aptitude, design competence, and the application of
theoretical knowledge to practical and research-oriented engineering problems.
15.7.1.2 The Dissertation Work shall be carried out in two stages, namely:
a) Dissertation Work Part-I during III Semester (II Year I Semester), and
b) Dissertation Work Part-II during IV Semester (II Year II Semester).
15.7.1.3 The minimum duration of the Dissertation Work shall be forty (40) weeks, spread over
two consecutive semesters.
15.7.2 Dissertation Review Committee (DRC):
15.7.2.1 A Dissertation Review Committee (DRC) shall be constituted for each programme

a) Head of the Department: Chairperson
b) Dissertation Supervisor: Member
c) One Senior Faculty Member of the Department: Member
15.7.3 Registration and Approval of Dissertation Work:
15.7.3.1 A student shall be permitted to register for Dissertation Work only after satisfying the
attendance requirements in all theory and laboratory courses.
15.7.3.2 Within four (4) weeks from the commencement of the III Semester (II Year I Semester),
the student, in consultation with the Dissertation Supervisor, shall present the title,
objectives, and plan of work before the DRC for approval. The student shall commence
the Dissertation Work only after obtaining such approval.
15.7.3.3 If a student desires to change the Dissertation Supervisor or the topic of work, such
change may be permitted only with the approval of the DRC. If the change results in a
major deviation from the originally approved proposal, the date of registration for
Dissertation Work shall be redefined from the date of approval of such change.
15.7.4 Progress Reviews and Duration:
15.7.4.1 The Dissertation Work shall commence at the beginning of the II Year and shall extend
over two semesters.
15.7.4.2 Each student shall submit periodic progress reports and present the work in at least
two stages, with a minimum gap of three (3) months between successive reviews.

15.7.5.1 Dissertation Work Review-II, conducted during III Semester, shall carry Continuous
Internal Evaluation (CIE) of 100 marks.
15.7.5.2 Evaluation shall be carried out as follows:
a) Dissertation Review Committee: 50 marks
b) Dissertation Supervisor: 50 marks
15.7.5.3 The assessment shall include problem definition, objectives, scope of work, literature
survey, methodology and progress achieved.

promoting curiosity, innovation and experiential learning among students.

to perform experiments that enhance conceptual understanding and strengthen
competence in the respective disciplines.
15.10.3 Students may utilize Virtual Laboratories to perform advanced or specialized
experiments that are not available in the Institute but are relevant for supplementing
learning in specific courses.

during the Programme. The selection of specific Virtual Laboratory courses or

15.10.5 Participation in Virtual Laboratory courses or experiments shall be optional and shall
be in addition to the mandatory laboratory courses prescribed in the curriculum.

evaluator and the revaluation result shall be declared within the stipulated timeline.
19.3 Revaluation is permitted only for theory courses. Revaluation shall not be permitted

24.0 SCHEME FOR THE AWARD OF DEGREE AND CLASS:
24.1 Registration and Credit Requirements:
24.1.1 A student shall register for and successfully complete all the courses prescribed in the
curriculum of the M.Tech. Programme.
24.1.2 A student shall earn a total of sixty-eight (68) credits as specified in the Course
Structure and shall secure a final CGPA of not less than 6.0.
24.2 Award of M.Tech. Degree:
24.2.1 A student who registers for all the prescribed courses, satisfies all the course
requirements, passes all the examinations prescribed for the Programme, and earns
the required number of credits with a CGPA ≥ 6.0, shall be declared to have qualified
for the award of the M.Tech. Degree in the branch of Engineering/Technology with the
specialization in which the student was admitted.
24.2.2 A student shall be eligible for the award of the M.Tech. Degree provided the
programme is completed within a minimum of two (2) academic years and a maximum
of four (4) academic years from the date of commencement of the first academic year.
24.2.3 A student who fails to fulfil all the academic requirements for the award of the Degree
within the stipulated maximum duration shall forfeit the seat in the M.Tech.
Programme.
24.3 Awarding of Class:
24.3.1 A student who qualifies for the award of the Degree as per Clause 24.2 shall be placed
in one of the following classes based on the final CGPA as shown in Table 10.
Table 10: CGPA and Class

≥ 6.5 and < 7.5 First Class
≥ 6.0 and < 6.5 Second Class
24.3.2 A student with a final CGPA less than 6.00 shall not be eligible for the award of the
M.Tech. Degree.
25.0 AWARD OF GOLD MEDAL:
25.1 The award of Gold Medal shall be governed by the norms approved by the Academic
Council and shall be separate from the award of class of the Degree.
25.2 Eligibility for the Gold Medal shall be restricted to students who have been awarded
First Class with Distinction, as defined under Clause 24.0 of these Regulations, subject
to additional criteria as approved by the Academic Council from time to time, as given
below:
32
(a) The student shall have secured the highest Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA)
among the eligible graduating students.
(b) The student has passed all courses in the first appearance (regular examinations
only) in the stipulated minimum duration.
(c) The student has not been detained at any stage of the programme due to shortage
of attendance.
(d) The student has not been subjected to any disciplinary action during the
programme.
25.3 In the event of a tie among eligible students, the following criteria shall be applied
sequentially for determining the Gold Medal recipient:
(a) Higher number of semesters with the highest SGPA.
(b) If still tied, the higher total number of ‘O’ (Outstanding) and ‘A+’ (Excellent) grades
secured across all courses.
(c) If still tied, the higher SGPA shall be considered sequentially from Semester I to
Semester IV.

26.0 RE-ADMISSION AND RE-REGISTRATION:
26.1 Re-Admission of a Discontinued Student:
26.1.1 A student who has discontinued the M.Tech. Degree Programme for any reason
whatsoever may be considered for re-admission into the same Degree Programme
with the same specialization, subject to the academic regulations in force at the time
of re-admission, with prior approval of the competent authorities.
26.1.2 Such re-admission shall be subject to the maximum permissible duration for
completion of the Programme as prescribed under these Regulations.
26.2 Re-Registration Due to Shortage of Attendance:
26.2.1 A student who is detained in any course(s) due to shortage of attendance in a semester
shall be required to re-register for the same course(s) when they are next offered.
26.2.2 If the same course is not offered, the student may be permitted to re-register for an
equivalent course, as recommended by the Board of Studies of the concerned
Department and approved by the competent authority.
26.2.3 Such students shall be governed by the Academic Regulations of the batch into which
they are re-registered.
26.3 Re-Registration Due to Failure in Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE):
26.3.1 A student who has failed in a course due to securing less than 40% marks in the
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) shall be permitted to re-register for that course in
the next academic year.
26.3.2 The re-registration for such course(s) shall be completed within four (4) weeks from
the commencement of classwork in the relevant semester.
26.3.3 Upon re-registration, the student shall be required to attend the classes afresh, secure
the prescribed minimum attendance, and complete all components of Continuous
Internal Evaluation and Semester End Examination anew.
26.3.4 In such cases, the Continuous Internal Evaluation marks and Semester End Examination
marks obtained in the earlier attempt shall stand cancelled and only the marks
obtained in the re-registered attempt shall be considered for grade computation,
provided the student secures a passing grade in the course.
26.3.5 There shall be no restriction on the number of courses for which a student may re-
register under this provision, subject to compliance with these Regulations.

27.1 Every student shall maintain discipline and decorum within and outside the Institute

27.2 Students shall strictly abide by all rules, guidelines, codes of conduct and instructions
issued by the Institute from time to time, including but not limited to:
a) Conduct in classrooms, laboratories, library and common areas
b) Dress code and identity card compliance
c) Restrictions on the use of mobile phones and electronic devices
d) Campus safety and security rules

27.3 Acts of Misconduct and Related Policies:
(a) Acts of Misconduct: Acts of misconduct such as ragging (as defined by UGC and
Anti-Ragging Regulations), physical or verbal abuse, sexual misconduct, harassment,
intimidation, fighting, threatening behaviour, discrimination, bullying (including cyber-
bullying), possession or consumption of alcohol or drugs, damage to property,
disorderly conduct, or disruption of academic or administrative activities shall attract
strict disciplinary action as per Institute rules and/or applicable statutory and legal
provisions.
(b) Drug-Free Campus Policy: The Institute shall maintain a Drug-Free Campus.
Possession, consumption, distribution, sale, or promotion of narcotic drugs,
psychotropic substances, tobacco products, or any intoxicating substances within the
campus or during any Institute-sponsored activity is strictly prohibited and shall be
dealt with in accordance with Institute disciplinary rules and applicable laws.
(c) Anti-Ragging Measures: Ragging in any form, whether physical, verbal,
psychological, written, or electronic, within or outside the Institute campus, is strictly
prohibited. Any student found guilty of ragging shall be punished as per the UGC
Regulations on Curbing the Menace of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions, as
amended from time to time, and the rules and procedures adopted by the Institute.

(Theory/Practical, CIE/SEE, Online/Offline) or during any academic activity shall be
liable for punitive action as per the Malpractice Rules as appended under these
Regulations.

statutory policies such as Anti-Ragging Regulations, Student Code of Conduct, Gender
Sensitization Regulations and Safety Policies. Violation of the same shall invite
disciplinary action.
27.6 The competent authority of the Institute shall determine the nature and extent of
disciplinary action and such decisions shall be final and binding on the student.

A1.0 APPLICABILITY:
A1.1 These rules and the prescribed punishments shall apply to any student who indulges

internal assessments, continuous evaluation and Semester End Examinations, whether
theory, practical, project viva-voce and online or offline, conducted by the Institution.
A1.2 Wherever the term student is not explicitly mentioned in subsequent clauses, it shall
be implicitly understood to refer to the student involved in the malpractice.
A1.3 In the case of internal assessments, including sessional examinations, the punishments
described in these rules shall be applied in a similar manner and adjusted to the
seriousness of the offence, limited to the concerned course, assessment component,
or Semester, as deemed appropriate by the competent authority.
A1.4 All question papers and examination materials, whether in physical or electronic form,
shall be treated as confidential. Unauthorized access or handling of such material by
students shall constitute malpractice, irrespective of whether the examination has
commenced.

offence, even if detected before actual execution.
A2.2 In deciding whether a student has committed malpractice, the Malpractice Committee
will review all the evidence and determine what is most likely to have happened. The
Committee does not need absolute certainty; action may be taken if the evidence
shows that malpractice probably occurred.
A2.3 Prior to the imposition of any punishment under these rules, the student concerned
shall be given an opportunity to present their explanation before the Malpractice
Committee. The Committee shall examine the case and submit its recommendation to
the competent authority for final decision.

A3.0 MALPRACTICES IDENTIFIED BY SQUAD / OBSERVER / SPECIAL INVIGILATORS:
A3.1 Punishments shall be imposed strictly in accordance with the above clauses.
A3.2 Malpractice Identified at the Spot Valuation Centre:
A3.2.1 The following procedure shall be followed in cases of malpractice detected during
valuation, scrutiny etc. at the Spot Valuation Centre:
i. The case shall be referred to the Malpractice Committee.
ii. A notice seeking an explanation shall be issued to the student(s) involved through
the Principal, at both the permanent address and the address for communication.
iii. If any examination-related staff (including, but not limited to, personnel involved
in conducting examinations, invigilation, evaluation, or maintenance of
examination records) is found involved, such conduct shall be viewed seriously and
suitable disciplinary action shall be initiated after due enquiry.
iv. Based on the explanation received and the Committee’s recommendation,
appropriate action shall be taken.
