About the event:
The Event HARDWARWE HACKATHON is conducted as part of the Convergence on 14th to
15th of December. The HARDWARE HACKATHON was a one-round competition aimed at
encouraging participants to express their technical knowledge and passion towards innovation that
could solve an existing problem.
No. of Participants: A total of 22 teams participated with 66 Students
Venue: A 209, A206, P 102/1
Timings: 9:00 a.m - 4:30 p.m

Objectives:
Innovative Approach: Provide a platform for brainstorming on different aspects in order to bring
a unique solution for an existing problem.
Nurture technical skills: Provide a platform for participants to hone their ability to utilize
complex technical concepts in solving a real-world problem in a more innovative manner.
Promote knowledge sharing: Encourage participants to share their understanding and insights
on various technical topics with a wider audience.
Increase technical awareness: Spark interest in technical subjects among participants and non-
technical audiences alike.
Enhance engagement: Add a creative and interactive element to the technical fest.
Event Format:

both the days.
On Day 1, Participants were allowed to work on their problem statements.
On Day 2, Participants have undergone an evaluation where several inputs were given by the CEO
of DRDO
On the same day after a stipulated period of time, another round of evaluation took place
accompanied by a set of jury members and implementations made by the participants considering
the inputs given before were keenly observed and taken into consideration.

SOFTWARE HACKATHON REPORT
About the Event:
Convergence, the annual tech fest of VNRVJIET, features a Software hackathon where teams across
diverse domains compete intensively for 24 hours, endeavoring to craft innovative solutions for
real-life problems. The event commenced with an Abstract submission phase, drawing participation
from 80 teams. Following a stringent selection process, 30 teams were chosen, and ultimately, 27
teams actively participated in the hackathon. During the event, three distinguished jury members,
esteemed alumni of VNRVJIET and seasoned professionals from the industry, undertook the
responsibility of evaluating the projects. The evaluation criteria were thoughtfully designed, with a
weighted evaluation matrix emphasizing ideation (30%), innovation (30%), and implementation
(40%) aspects. Evaluation was conducted following robust rubrics and assessment methods. The
hackathon provided a platform for participants to showcase their problem-solving prowess,
fostering an environment where creativity, innovation, and technical expertise converged to create
impactful solutions.
No of Participants: 27 Teams.
Venue: A 109, A110, A112, A113, Patron’s Bhavan, VNRVJIET

CODING CONTEST:
We have conducted the Coding Contest as a part of Convergence 2k23. The event consisted of 2
coding rounds i.e Qualifier round and Final round. These rounds were conducted on HackerRank
platform and both rounds consisted of 6 coding questions. Participants are required to form a team
of size maximum 3 members. The deadline for registering for the qualifier round was on 10th
December, 2023.
Qualifier Round:
The qualifier round was conducted online on 11th December, 2023. The duration of the qualifier
round was 2hrs from 7pm to 9pm. 483 teams registered for the contest, out of which 240 teams
were promoted to the final round after thorough scrutiny.
Final Round:
The final round was conducted on 15th December, 2023 in VNRVJIET labs. The duration of the
final round was 2hrs 30mins from 10:30am to 1pm. Out of the registered 240 teams, 24 teams
participated in the final round. The top 3 performers of the final round were declared as winners.
The winners won the cash prize with a pool of 12000/-.

CAMPUS AUTOMATION CONTEST REPORT
About the Event:
The Team GDSC (Google Developer Student Club) and CSI (Computer Society of India) have
organised a CAMPUS AUTOMATION contest, to enhance the knowledge of students in the field
of integration of technology within the campus to automate it. It was a two days hackathon. The
goal is to empower participants to create solutions that contribute to a more efficient, accessible,
and sustainable campus, enriching the overall experience for students, faculty, and staff. By
implementing campus automation systems, colleges can create a centralized database that
facilitates easy access to information for both students and administrators.
We focused on addressing campus challenges through automation and innovation, the participants
brought their A-game to the event. Their commitment and energy were evident as they
collaboratively worked to develop creative prototypes that showcased inventive solutions to
streamline campus operations. The prototypes presented not only demonstrated technical prowess
but also reflected a keen understanding of the campus environment and a commitment to
improving it. The event encapsulated a dynamic blend of teamwork, creativity, and technological
prowess, leaving a lasting impact on the campus community and setting the stage for future
advancements in campus automation.

software and tools in creating innovative designs. The event spans over a specified duration,

CADATON aims to cultivate a community of skilled designers, fostering an environment where
creativity meets technical proficiency. The event not only enhances participants' CAD skills but
also serves as a platform for networking, knowledge sharing, and the exploration of innovative

CAPTURE THE FLAG (CTF) REPORT
About the Event:
Capture the Flag, a highly anticipated and dynamic event, unfolded with great enthusiasm and
excitement, bringing together students from all academic years for a day of spirited competition.
This engaging activity, consisting of two rounds, proved to be a perfect blend of strategy, teamwork,
and sheer fun, with a unique twist focusing on flag discovery rather than direct attacks.
Objectives:
Promoting Inclusivity: By inviting students from all academic years, the event aimed to create a
diverse and vibrant atmosphere, fostering connections among participants who might not typically
interact.
Encouraging Teamwork: The heart of the event lay in promoting teamwork and collaboration.
Participants were encouraged to work together to navigate through the designated territory,
fostering a sense of unity and camaraderie.
Providing a Learning Experience: The Qualifier Round served as an initial challenge, allowing
teams to familiarize themselves with the game dynamics. This provided a valuable learning
experience, helping participants understand their strengths and areas for improvement.
Elevating Competition: The Final Round, with its heightened stakes and advanced challenges,
aimed to elevate the competition, pushing participants to showcase their best strategic thinking and
problem-solving skills.
Event Format:
Capture the Flag featured a carefully structured two-round format:
Qualifier Round: The event kicked off with the Qualifier Round, where teams engaged in the initial
challenges of flag discovery. This round served as a warm-up, allowing participants to learn the
game dynamics and providing insights into their performance.
Final Round: Only the top-performing teams from the Qualifier Round advanced to the Final
Round. This round raised the stakes, requiring participants to employ advanced strategies and tactics
in the ultimate flag discovery showdown. The heightened competition added an extra layer of
excitement to the event.
Capture The Flag team has conducted intramurals on 12th December 2023, at B Block Seminar
hall from 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM.
The key takeaways from the participants who have attended are:
● Understood what Capture The Flag contest is about
● Learnt key concepts to tackle the problems
● Understood how to approach a problem statement till we reach the flag
● Clarified their doubts

Valorant LAN Contest Report
Valorant is a team-based first-person tactical hero shooter set in the near future. Players play as
one of a set of Agents, characters based on several countries and cultures around the world. In the
main game mode, players are assigned to either the attacking or defending team with each team
having five players on it.
It’s a 5v5 multiplayer first-person shooter (FPS) where one team attacks and the other defends.
The main game mode, Search and Destroy, is very similar to CS:GO. The attacking team’s goal is
to plant a bomb (called a spike) and have it detonate, while the defending team tries to avoid that.
Regardless of whether the spike is planted or not, if a squad is wiped out before any other victory
condition is met, the opposing squad will win.

AUTO EXPO 2023 REPORT
About the Event:
Convergence2k23R lived up to its promise of hosting one of the most exhilarating automotive
events of the year with its Auto Expo. Spread across two days, the event witnessed an impressive
showcase of vehicles, stunts, and enthusiasm.
Day 1 Highlights: 14th December
Vehicle Display: The football ground transformed into a haven for automotive enthusiasts. Vintage
vehicles gleamed under the sun, supercars showcased their power, and tuned cars displayed their
unique modifications. The presence of bikes, especially the ones from Srujan Bharathwaj, a notable
YouTube creator from the channel 2brothersvlogs and an esteemed alumni of VNRVJIET in civil
engineering, added an extra layer of excitement.
Special Appearance: Srujan Bharathwaj's presence added a personal touch to the event. His bikes,
combined with his YouTube influence, drew significant attention from the attendees, sparking
conversations and discussions about the evolving world of biking and content creation.
Day 2 Highlights: 15th December
Stunt Showcase: The basketball court became the epicenter of adrenaline-pumping action as Imran
Rider Stunt Academy took the stage. Their bike stunts were nothing short of spectacular,
showcasing precision, skill, and courage.
Footfall & Participation: Despite being a weekday, the event garnered a commendable footfall of
800-1000 students. Their enthusiasm and energy added vibrancy to the expo, making it a memorable
experience for everyone present.
Engagement & Feedback: The audience was actively engaged, cheering for the performers and
interacting with the organizers. The positive feedback received post-event was a testament to its
success and the organizers' efforts in curating a memorable experience.
Conclusion:
The Auto Expo at Convergence2k23R was a resounding success, celebrating the world of
automobiles and stunts. The meticulous planning, combined with the support of sponsors like
Gobikers and the participation of esteemed personalities like Srujan Barathwaj, ensured that
attendees received a holistic and exhilarating experience.
The event not only showcased the best of vehicles and stunts but also fostered a sense of community
among automotive enthusiasts. As the sun set on the second day, the echoes of bike engines and the
cheers of the crowd remained, serving as a reminder of the unforgettable moments and memories
created during Convergence2k23R's Auto Expo.

BLOG-A-THON REPORT
About the Event:
The Event BLOG-A-THON is conducted as part of the Convergence on the 14th, and
15th of December. The BLOG-A-THON was a two-round competition aimed at
encouraging participants to express their technical knowledge and passion through
creative writing.
Objectives:
 Nurture technical writing skills: Provide a platform for participants to hone their
ability to communicate complex technical concepts in a clear, engaging, and creative
manner.
 Promote knowledge sharing: Encourage participants to share their understanding and
insights on various technical topics with a wider audience.
 Increase technical awareness: Spark interest in technical subjects among
participants and non-technical audiences alike.
 Enhance engagement: Add a creative and interactive element to the technical fest.
Event Format:
The blog-a-thon was a two-round competition, with each round focusing on an aspect of
technical writing.
Round 1: (Pre-Fest Online Submission)
 Participants were given two domains; Software and Hardware was also allowed.
 Deadline for Submission was 14th of December 11:00am
 They were required to submit original blog posts within a set word limit, showcasing
their writing skills and understanding of the theme.
 A panel of judges evaluated the submissions based on criteria such as content, styling,
content, and verbal context.
Round 2: On-the-Spot Writing (Fest Day Competition)
 Shortlisted participants from Round 1 were invited to participate in a live writing
competition during the fest.
 They were provided with new topics and given a limited time to write a blog post.
 Judges assessed the participants' writing skills under pressure, adaptability, and ability
to communicate effectively in a fast-paced environment.
