ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT
The Department was established in 2020 with B.Tech. Data Science and Cyber Security
programmes with an intake of 60 in each programme. In 2021, B.Tech. The AI & DS
programme started with an intake of 60, while the Data Science intake increased to 120.
The Department is dedicated to exploring the emerging fields of Cyber Security, Data
Science, and Artificial Intelligence. The intake of B.Tech. Data Science program has
been increased to 180 in the academic year 2023–24.
The Department is involved in pioneering research and supports research activities both
for faculty members and students. The Department nurtures the culture of innovation,
encouraging students and faculty members to explore new ideas.
The Department engages in research projects that also involve students. The research
output is published in the form of papers in reputed journals and conferences. The Thrust
areas of cybersecurity are cloud security, malware protection, network and application
security, and data security.
The Department of CSE (CyS, DS) and AI&DS offers a minor degree in Cyber Security at
VNR VJIET, equips students with fundamental skills in protecting information systems,
covering areas like network security, cryptography, and cybersecurity protocols.
Lifelong learning skill is encouraged among faculty and students. The focus is on learning
cutting-edge technologies. Learning is also encouraged through domain-related
professional clubs, namely - VJ DATA QUESTERS, VJ GARUDA VIGILANCE, and VJ ARC.

FACULTY EXPERTISE
The faculty at the Department of Cyber Security is a formidable ensemble of seasoned

landscape of Cyber Security.

STUDENT CLUB - VJ GARUDA VIGILANCE

innovating in the intricate domain of digital fortification. Established with the overarching goal of incubating a

THRUST AREA OF RESEARCH
The Cyber Security department is a hub of innovative research, where faculty and students collaborate on cutting-
edge projects. From developing advanced cryptographic algorithms to exploring novel data security techniques,
the department is at the forefront of pushing the boundaries of knowledge in the field. Research works delve into
diverse areas, including Network and Cloud Security, Cryptography, Cryptanalysis and Malware analysis.
This vibrant research environment not only contributes to the academic landscape but also fosters a culture of
continuous learning and exploration within the Cyber Security community.The Cyber Security domain actively
fosters a culture of research, promoting and supporting innovative works that contribute to advancements in
the field.
