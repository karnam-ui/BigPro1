Memory Perspective of Indian Diasporic Theatre
Funded by ICSSR- Project Director-Dr. D. Sudha Rani
Project Value-10 lacs Duration-12 months 2025-26
The cultural memory of the diaspora community plays a crucial role in defining and shaping the
cultural identity of its individuals and the community. Literature and more importantly drama
along with other performing arts of the diasporic community are major sources to comprehend
their cultural identity. Indian diaspora is present in many countries like UK, Canada, South Africa,
and their presence in United States of America is of recognizable size. The Indian diaspora, found
in countries like the UK, Canada, South Africa, and the USA, has produced a considerable body
of literature, though theatre has less recognition due to lack of many published anthologies and it
began its evolution barely three decades ago. In recent decades, numerous theatre groups and
playwrights from the Indian diaspora have emerged, gaining recognition. Therefore, the present
project attempts to do a complete and comprehensive survey of Indian diasporic plays, playgroups,
theatre companies working across the United States of America and United Kingdom of Great
Britain where Indian diaspora theatre is prominent (for want of time, the researcher cannot consider
more countries). And then, compile and publish an annotated bibliography of popular and
frequently staged and English plays, details of playgroups, theatre companies and movements.
After this, the researcher would like to select a few significant plays from both USA and UK for a
detailed analysis of the major issues of Indian diasporic ‘self’ and all the related issues. This project
also gives us an opportunity to analyze whether the issues related to Indian diaspora in USA and
UK change in the past three decades. For the detailed study of these plays, the researcher would
employ cultural memory theories, theories related diaspora, sociology, anthropology, psychology
to understand the nexus of Indian diaspora, cultural identity and homeland and analyse their
experiences.
