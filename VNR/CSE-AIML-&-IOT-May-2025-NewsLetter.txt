HoD MESSAGE
Dear Students, Faculty, and Readers,
It is with great pride and enthusiasm that I welcome you
to the May-2025 edition of our newsletter “Digital
Dynamos” the official newsletter of the Department of
CSE-Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning &
Internet of Things (AIML & IoT). In this rapidly evolving
technological era, AIML & IoT are shaping the future,
transforming industries, and redefining the way we
interact with the world. Our department remains
committed to fostering innovation, research, and
excellence in these domains.
Through this newsletter, we aim to highlight the
remarkable achievements of our students and faculty,
ongoing research initiatives, industrial collaborations,
and recent advancements in the field. I encourage all
students and faculty members to actively participate,
contribute, and explore the endless possibilities that
AIML & IoT offer.
Let us continue to learn, innovate, and lead with passion
and commitment. Looking forward to an exciting
journey ahead!
Dr. Sagar Yeruva
Head of Department
Department of CSE-AIML & IoT

Department Highlights
Dr. A. Harshvardhan, Senior Assistant Professor, serves as the spokesperson and coordinator. Ms.
M. Shara Lydia, Assistant Professor, is the branch counselor. They attended admission activities this
month, working every Tuesday and Saturday to fulfill their responsibilities. They have been
effectively communicating with parents regarding the admission process.
On 3rd May 2025, final-year students from AIML-A, B & IoT streams showcased their Major
Projects as part of the "Show and Tell" event. Each section, comprising 18 batches, participated
enthusiastically. The initiative significantly contributed to the development of problem-solving,
research, and teamwork abilities among students.
The AIML-C stream final-year students presented their Major Projects on 5th May 2025, with 18
batches taking part. This presentation provided students with an opportunity to explore potential
publications, patents, or further research avenues.
On 23rd May 2025, third-year second-semester students involved in Summer Internships related
to IoT shared their learning experiences. A total of 72 students participated, gaining practical
experience and building industry connections that may open doors to future job opportunities.
Continuing the initiative, on 24th May 2025, 208 students from AIML-A, B & C (third-year, second
semester) highlighted their experiences from their summer internships. The session helped them
gain insight into specific industries or roles, while also encouraging proactive learning and hands-
on experience.

Research updates and Publications

Machine Learning Approach for Differentiating Spindle Cell and Giant Cell Carcinomas and
Assessing Severity Levels” at the 8th International Conference on Electronics, Materials
Engineering & Nanotechnology (IEMENTech 2025) on 16th April 2025. The paper is indexed in
IEEE.
Dr. G. Nagaraju, Assistant Professor, presented “Development of Telugu Treebank Using Panini
Karaka Relations for Dependency Parsing” at the 15th International Conference on Soft
Computing and Pattern Recognition (SoCPaR 2023) on 4th May 2025, and it is indexed in Springer.
Ms. Preethy Singh, Assistant Professor, presented her work “Automated News Summarization
using Transformers” at the International Conference on ISML 2024 on 23rd May 2025, with the
paper being indexed in IEEE.
Mr. Shaik Mabasha, Assistant Professor, contributed to the presentation of “A Machine Learning-
Based Framework for Drought Prediction and Resilience in Wheat Cultivation” at the International
Conference on Signal Processing Communication Power and Embedded System (SCOPES-2024)
on 16th May 2025, indexed in IEEE.
Ms. Jyotsnarani Tripathy, Assistant Professor, authored “Predictive Modelling of Parkinson’s
Disease Progression Using LightGBM Classifier”, published in the Journal of Neonatal Surgery (Vol.
14, No. 20S, 2025) on 1st May 2025. The article is indexed in Scopus (Q4).
Mr. D. Bhupesh, Assistant Professor, published “A Performance-driven Evaluation of Deep Learning
for Concrete Crack Detection with Varying Dataset Sizes and Training Epochs: Real-World
Implications for Infrastructure Monitoring” in the Asian Journal of Civil Engineering on 15th May
2025, indexed in Springer.
Ms. M. Swapna Kumari, Assistant Professor, co-authored the paper “Deep Learning-Based Early
Detection of Rare Diseases,” published in the Journal of Neonatal Surgery (Vol. 14, No. 14S, 2025),
indexed in Scopus (Q3).
Mr. V. Kishore, Assistant Professor, along with co-authors, published “Interpretable Machine
Learning Models with Attention-Based Feature Attribution for High-Dimensional Tabular Data” in
the Journal of Information Systems Engineering and Management (May 2025), indexed in Scopus.
