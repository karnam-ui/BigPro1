Best Practice-1
1. Title of the Practice
Moderation of Internal evaluation question papers
2. Goal
 Enhance Quality and Alignment:
Ensure that question papers are aligned with the course outcomes, syllabus, and
cognitive levels defined by Bloom's Taxonomy, thereby fostering comprehensive
evaluation of students' knowledge, application, and analytical skills.
 Promote Consistency and Fairness:
Maintain consistency in the level of difficulty, distribution of marks, and clarity in the
framing of questions to provide an equitable assessment environment for all students.
 Facilitate Transparency and Accountability:
Establish a systematic review process that involves subject experts, ensuring the
inclusion of relevant and meaningful questions while eliminating ambiguity and
redundancy.
 Encourage Continuous Improvement:
Identify gaps or potential areas for improvement in question paper design through
feedback from the moderation committee and incorporate them into subsequent
evaluations.
3. The Context
VNRVJIET is committed to achieving academic excellence through robust teaching-learning
processes and quality assurance mechanisms. As an institution accredited by NAAC for three
consecutive cycles, VNRVJIET emphasizes maintaining high standards in all academic
activities, including internal evaluations.
Internal evaluation plays a pivotal role in assessing students' understanding, application, and
analytical skills during the learning process. To ensure that the internal evaluation question
papers are of high quality, aligned with course outcomes, and free from bias or ambiguity,
the institution has implemented a systematic moderation process.
4. The Practice
.
The moderation of question papers involves a structured review by subject experts and
senior faculty members, who evaluate the content for relevance, clarity, and adherence to
the syllabus and Bloom's Taxonomy. This practice aims to address common challenges such
as:
 Discrepancies in the level of difficulty across sections or courses.
 Ambiguity or redundancy in the framing of questions.
 Mismatched or disproportionate allocation of marks to questions.
 Misalignment with course outcomes or cognitive levels.
By institutionalizing this best practice, VNRVJIET ensures that internal evaluations are fair,
transparent, and effective in measuring students' competencies. It reflects the institution's
dedication to continuous improvement and its mission to provide quality education that
meets global standards.
5. Evidence of Success
The implementation of the moderation process for internal evaluation question papers at VNRVJIET
has yielded significant improvements in the quality and effectiveness of assessments. The following
evidence highlights the success of this best practice:
Improved Question Paper Quality:
o A marked improvement in the alignment of questions with the course outcomes and
syllabus.
o Elimination of errors, ambiguities, and redundancies in question papers.
o Balanced distribution of questions across cognitive levels, as defined by Bloom's
Taxonomy.
2. Enhanced Student Performance and Feedback:
o Students have demonstrated a better understanding of the examination expectations,
leading to improved academic performance.
o Positive feedback from students and faculty regarding the clarity and relevance of the
questions.
3. Increased Faculty Awareness and Collaboration:
o Faculty members have gained deeper insights into effective question paper design and
evaluation strategies through their involvement in the moderation process.
o Strengthened interdisciplinary collaboration among faculty during the review and
moderation process.
4. Alignment with Institutional Goals:
o The practice has supported the institution’s commitment to continuous quality
improvement and its mission to provide a rigorous and transparent assessment
system.
Overall, this best practice has elevated the academic standards of internal evaluations at VNRVJIET,
contributing to a culture of quality and excellence in education.

High intensity training for Core engineering and Computer science allied engineering students
2. Goal
The goal is to equip students with industry-relevant skills, enhance their problem-solving
capabilities, and prepare them to meet the demands of a rapidly evolving technological
landscape. This practice aims to Bridge the Industry-Academia Gap, Develop Technical
Proficiency, Foster Innovation and Problem-Solving Skills, Encourage Lifelong Learning,
Enhance Multidisciplinary Collaboration.
3. The Context
.
In today’s dynamic and technology-driven world, engineering education must go beyond
traditional classroom teaching to meet the demands of industry and society. Core engineering
disciplines, such as Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, and allied fields in Computer Science, are
increasingly converging, requiring students to possess a strong foundation in their domains
while also being adept at interdisciplinary applications.
At VNRVJIET, the rapidly changing technological landscape and growing expectations from
employers have highlighted the need for intensive skill development programs.
