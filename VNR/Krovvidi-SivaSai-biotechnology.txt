C. Contribution to Science
1) Infectious Diseases – LEPROSY
a) Recombinant Interferon-Gamma Phase – I Clinical trials in Leprosy patients: One of
the mechanism identified for lack of macrophage response in leprosy patients is due to
lack of sufficient production of interferon-gamma by T cells in response to M.leprae
antigens. We carried out Clinical trials using Human recombinant interferon-gamma in
leprosy patients in association with Dr. Zohnvil Cohn from Rockefeller group. We showed
that IFN-gamma is effective at localized positions in decreasing the bacterial load and
enhancing the oxidative stress in macrophages in TT and BT cases for some time
however it has little impact in a systemic form of lepromatous leprosy individuals.

61(2): 259-264 {Citations: 14, IF: 0.22, h-index: 05}

b) Assessed the role of TLR polymorphism in leprosy disease
Case control study suggested that Genetic association of G896A polymorphism of TLR-4
gene in leprosy confers susceptibility, indicating that contacts with AA genotype may be
of higher risk even though there is no difference in the frequency of genotypes in healthy
contacts and leprosy patients. Other study aim is to evaluate the influence of GT
microsatellite on the expression of TLR-2 which could make humans prone to M.leprae
infections. We found that allele/genotype of TLR2 microsatellite which includes longer GT
repeats was associated with low TLR-2 mRNA expression and high IL-10 production
which might predispose house hold contacts to leprosy.

2.14, h-index 75, citations-2}
4. Naveen CS, Venkata SKN, Kamakshi PD, Suman J, SivaSai KSR, Vijayalakshmi V,

Gene in Leprosy through Family-based and Case-Control Study Designs. Trans R Soc Trop

c) Explored soil and water could be a source of live M. leprae.
The exact mode of transmission of M.leprae is not clearly understood. In this regard soil,
water, house hold contacts, patients samples were collected from individuals and soil and
water from different surrounding areas and far way places where patients reside. Our
study suggested that viable M.leprae were present in soil and water samples where the
patient reside and the type of M.leprae is same found in all the sources indicating the
possibility of soil and water could be a potential reservoirs of M. leprae.

2) Tuberculosis – Pulmonary TB – Lung TB
a) Characterization of two novel Mycobacterium tuberculosis genes Rv0679c &
Rv0180c and their protein products in pathogenesis of tuberculosis (TB).
The antigens were shown to have a role in invasion using macrophage and epithelial cell
lines in in vitro experiments. However, no studies were reported from patients. Our molecular
analysis of Rv0180c in clinical isolates of tuberculosis revealed an SNP at 258th position G to
T was identified in the gene, which is the first report globally. We also assessed the role of
TLRs and their interaction with novel invasive proteins and their peptides using
bioinformatics approach. Further studies are ongoing in assessing the immune-dominance of
the proteins in terms of B and T cell responses in tuberculosis patients.

b) Assessment of HIV and Tuberculosis Co-infection studies and its Impact
We assessed the effect of HIV and Tuberculosis co-infection in patients with latent TB. Our
collaborative studies indicated the TLR-2 expression in HIV+ individuals with latent
tuberculosis infection is normal but the defective part is the signaling of MyD88 and IRAK4.
We also showed that IL-2 and IL-1beta production in HIV-Tuberculosis Co-infection is
suppressed by Transforming Growth Factor-beta which is adding to susceptibility. In HIV+
individuals with latent tuberculosis infection patients we assessed and found that memory
like natural killer cells are defective in terms of expansion and function.
