A Report on Entrepreneurship Development Cell, & Vignana Jyothi Technology
Business Incubator (VJTBI) Activities for the
Month of April, 2018
I) ED Cell Activities:
1) World Intellectual Property Day-2018 celebrations were jointly organized by ED Cell and
RCC at B-Block Seminar Hall, Patrons Bhavan on 26-04-2017 during 3 PM to 5 PM, in
which 15 faculty and 35 students of various branches of B.Tech and M.Tech have
participated. This year’s theme of the World IP Day is “Powering change for
women in innovation and creativity”.
Dr. A.S. Rao, the Institute’s Director for Innovation, Incubation, Entrepreneurship, and
Globalization, has given a power point presentation on the evolution of Intellectual
Property and also pointed that India could not play a role in the 1st Industrial Revolution
and that this generation can blame it on colonialism and added that the next generation
will not excuse us if we do not partake/Contribute in the 4th Industrial Revolution that is
around us today. He said that IP is a clear indicator for India’s participation and that the
youth today must thus work on innovative ideas and not simply fall for employment like
the previous generations.
The Institute's Director for Advancement, Dr. B. ChennaKesava Rao, also spoke at the
occasion and expressed his happiness that 13 patents were filed by the members of the
Institute and elucidated how the Institute encourages innovation in every aspect.
Dr. S. Rama Rao, Former Director of WIPO Coordination Office at New York City, USA
graced the event as the Chief Guest. Speaking at the occasion, Chief Guest Dr. Rama
Rao explained that World IP Organization (WIPO) is an organ of the United Nations
Organization that focuses on Intellectual Property (IP) like WHO focuses on Health.
Dr. Rama Rao called out to the youth to move from fundamental research to applied
research to innovation to productification to commercialization, clarifying that countries like
the USA and China are way ahead of India in manufacturing and the alternative route for
India’s development is through Science, Technology, and Innovation (STI) which is driven
by IP and thus guarantees marketability. Dr. Rama Rao concluded that his life is to excite
the youth and women to do something innovative and that India is capable of doing that.
Dr. V. Balakista Reddy, Professor of Law and Registrar at NALSAR University, Hyderabad
attended the event as the Guest of Honour. He addressed the audience on the occasion
and said that Indians focus very little in IP or protecting it. He said that India had been
helping the works with its human resources and intellect, which is all IP, and thus that India
has a responsibility to protect its own IP while supplying skilled breaks to the rest of the
world.
