HoD MESSAGE
Dear Students, Faculty, and Readers,
It is with great pride and enthusiasm that I welcome you
to the October-2025 edition of our newsletter “Digital
Dynamos” the official newsletter of the Department of
CSE-Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning &
Internet of Things (AIML & IoT). In this rapidly evolving
technological era, AIML & IoT are shaping the future,
transforming industries, and redefining the way we
interact with the world. Our department remains
committed to fostering innovation, research, and
excellence in these domains.
Through this newsletter, we aim to highlight the
remarkable achievements of our students and faculty,
ongoing research initiatives, industrial collaborations, and
recent advancements in the field. I encourage all students
and faculty members to actively participate, contribute,
and explore the endless possibilities that AIML & IoT
offer.
Let us continue to learn, innovate, and lead with passion
and commitment. Looking forward to an exciting journey
ahead!
Dr. Sagar Yeruva
Head of Department
Department of CSE-AIML & IoT

Department Highlights
The Department of CSE-AIML & IoT organized a coding contest KRITHOHUNT (Seek. Solve.
Succeed), on October 8, 2025. The event was convened by Dr. Sagar Yeruva, Associate
Professor and Head of the Department, with faculty coordinators Dr. A. Kousar Nikhath and Mr.
D. Bhupesh.
A guest lecture was organised on the topic “Complexity Classes: P, NP, NP-Complete and NP-
Hard,” which was delivered by Mr. Abdul Bari Mohammed, CEO of NBH Infotech, on October 11,
2025, at VNRVJIET. The faculty coordinators of the event are Dr. A. Harshavardhan, Senior
Assistant Professor Mr. K. Kishan Babu , Assistant Professor, Mr. U. Veeresh, Assistant Professor,
and Ms. M. Sharanya , Assistant Professor.
The IEEE VNRVJIET CIS, in collaboration with the Department of CSE -AIML & IoT, conducted an
IEEE Membership Drive on October 11, 2025. Dr. Y. Sagar, Associate Professor and Head of the
Department, and Mr. U. Veeresh, Assistant Professor, served as faculty coordinator for the
event.
The company ALFAGO initiated a Project-Based Learning (PBL) program for third-year students
(Batch 1, comprising 60 members) under a new MoU sanctioned on October 16, 2025. The
program is being guided by Dr. Y. Sagar Yeruva (HoD, CSE–AIML & IoT), Dr. N. Sandhya (RDC
Head & Professor), Dr. A. Kousar Nikhath, Dr. A. Harshavardhan, Ms. N. Haritha, and
Mr. D. Bhupesh.
The Department of CSE -AIML & IoT organized “Top 100 Coders: Enter the Codeverse of
Champions” under the convenorship of Dr. Sagar Yeruva, with Dr. A. Kousar Nikhath and
Mr. D. Bupesh as faculty coordinators. Round 1 of the event was conducted online on October
16, 2025, followed by Round 2 held on campus on October 22, 2025.
A guest lecture on “Normalization and Query Optimization” was organized in the department,
delivered by Mr. Vishwanath Arabati, Corporate Trainer, on October 24, 2025.The coordinators
of the event are Dr. K. Archana, Assistant Professor; Ms. Y. Prasanthi, Assistant Professor; and
Ms. V. Manjula, Assistant Professor
An alumni guest lecture titled “Bridging Academia and Industry: Implementing MLOps in Real-
World Projects” was organized on October 24, 2025. Distinguished alumni Mr. Vasista P. R. K.
(Ericsson R&D, TCS) and Ms. Kavya Miriyala (JP Morgan) shared valuable insights connecting
academic learning with industry practices. The session was coordinated by Ms. Preety Singh,
Assistant Professor.
A hands-on workshop on “Build and Deploy a Multimodal Assistant on the Cloud using Gemini
and Cloud Run” was conducted on October 25, 2025, at VNRVJIET. The workshop was led by Mr.
Ashutosh S. Bhakare, CEO of Unnati Development & Training Centre Pvt. Ltd., and Mr. Jay
Thakkar, AI Engineer at Impulsive Web.

Department Highlights
A three-day workshop on “Unlocking the Power of GEO-AI” was organized by the IEEE CIS/GRSS
Hyderabad Joint Chapter in collaboration with VNRVJIET and the Departments of Civil and CSE
(AIML & IoT), held from October 30 to November 1, 2025. Mr. U. Veeresh, Assistant Professor,
served as the faculty coordinator for the event.
Between August and September 2025, a research team successfully developed a repository
titled “Telugu SMILE Corpus for Multimodal Humor Understanding.” The project was led by
Dr. M. Radhika and Ms. A. Kaveri from IIIT Hyderabad, in collaboration with Dr. A. Kousar
Nikhath, Dr. G. Nagaraju, and Mr. V. Kishore from the CSE (AIML & IoT) departments at VNRVJIET.
Faculty members from the CSE–AIML & IoT departments participated in a one-week Staff
Development Program on “Hands-On ICT and Network Troubleshooting,” organized from
October 27 to November 1, 2025, at VNRVJIET.
Parent–Teacher Meetings for all years of CSE (AIML & IoT) were successfully conducted on
October 11, 2025. Class coordinators and mentors of the second, third, and fourth years
participated actively, discussing student performance, academic progress, and placement
preparedness with parents.
As part of the ISOC–Outreach initiative, Dr. Sayanti Chatterjee, Mr. V. Kishore, Ms. N. Haritha,
and Ms. M. Sharanya, Assistant Professors, visited Brahmanapally village on October 22, 2025.
During their visit, they donated a Samsung TV to pre-primary students and carried out
educational and community development activities aimed at enhancing rural learning
environments.

Research updates and Publications
Dr. A. Kousar Nikhath, Associate Professor, along with Ms. J. Ananya Reddy, Ms. P. Aishwarya,
Ms. S. Maadhurya Sri, and Ms. P. Gowthami, presented a paper titled “A Multilingual Deep
Learning Approach for Sign Language Recognition” at the 10th International Conference on
Smart Trends for Computing and Communications on 01 October 2025. The paper was
published and is indexed in Scopus.
Dr. A. Kousar Nikhath, Associate Professor, together with Ms. Aanchal Jain, Ms. D. Ananya, and
Mr. Ramana Teja, presented a paper titled “Deep Learning Model for Lip-Based Speech
Synthesis Conference” at the 10th International Conference on Smart Trends for Computing
and Communications on 01 October 2025. The paper was published and is indexed in Scopus.
Ms. Preety Singh, Assistant Professor, along with Ms. K.N.D. Saile and Ms. Sayeedakhanum
Pathan, presented a paper titled “Machine Learning-Based Prediction of Heart Attack Risk: A
Data-Driven Approach” at the 9th International Conference on IoT in Social, Mobile, Analytics,
and Cloud (I-SMAC 2025) held from 08–10 October 2025. The paper was published and is
indexed in Scopus.
Ms. P. Swetha, Assistant Professor, along with Ms. K. Prathyusha and Ms. S. Mamatha, presented
a paper titled “Immense Quality Thermal Image Analysis by ResNet and Deep Learning
Techniques” at the 1st International Conference on Advancements in Sustainable Energy,
Materials and Manufacturing Technology (ICASMMT 2024) on 30 September 2025. The paper
was published and is indexed in Scopus.
Ms. K. Prathyusha, Assistant Professor, along with Ms. T. Aruna, presented a paper titled
“Optimizing Disaster Recovery Processes Using Satellite Imaging and U-Net Architecture” at the
1st International Conference on Advancements in Sustainable Energy, Materials and
Manufacturing Technology (ICASMMT 2024) on 30 September 2025. The paper was published
and is indexed in Scopus.
Ms. Jyotsnarani Tripathi, Assistant Professor, along with Ms. T. Aruna Sri, Mr. T. Murali Krishna,
Mr. Manmath Nath Das, Ms. Sharada Mani, Mr. G. Laksmi Vara Prasad, and Ms. T. Manyam,
published a paper titled “An Adaptive AI-Driven Cyber Threat Detection Framework for
Securing Heterogeneous IoT Networks” in the Engineering, Technology & Applied Science
Research journal, Volume 15, Issue 5, pages 26750–26756, in October 2025. The paper has a Q2
impact factor and is indexed in Scopus.
Dr. A. Harshavardhan, Senior Assistant Professor, together with Mr. V. Hari Prasad, Dr. T. Bhaskar,
Ms. Sukanya Ledalla, and Mr. M. Varaprasad Rao, published a paper titled “NEUROEXPLAIN AI:
An Explainable AI and Statistical Framework for Brain Tumor” in the Journal of Theoretical and
Applied Information Technology, pages 25–25, on 15 September 2025. The paper has ISSN: 1817-
3195, a Q3 impact factor, and is indexed in Scopus.
