Digital Dynamos February 2026
Department Highlights
Ms. Vaidehi Ramadugu joined the Department of CSE (AIML & IoT), R&AI as an Assistant
Professor on 13 February 2026, strengthening the department’s academic team with her
expertise in computer science and artificial intelligence.
Dr. C. Naveen Reddy, Assistant Professor, was awarded a Ph.D. on 07 February 2026, marking a
significant academic achievement and contributing to the department’s growing research
strength.
The Department of CSE (AIML & IoT), R&AI successfully organized the ISOC Academic Hub
event “Safer Internet Day – Digital Safety & Awareness” on 10 February 2026. The session
focused on critical topics such as UPI scams, AI fraud, and digital footprints, creating
awareness among students about safe and responsible digital practices.
The department organized AI Week 2026 – “Engineering the Next Intelligence” from 9–13
February 2026, which provided valuable exposure to cutting-edge developments in Artificial
Intelligence and Responsible AI. The event benefited 220 participants and inspired students
to explore innovative AI-driven solutions.
An invited lecture titled “Machine Learning for Ground Robots” was delivered by Prof. Joyit
Mukherjee from BITS Pilani Hyderabad Campus on 11 February 2026. The session highlighted
the role of machine learning in enabling autonomous robotic systems across domains such as
defense, agriculture, logistics, and smart mobility, benefiting 160 participants.
A Placement Readiness Program (PRP) – Profile Building Workshop was conducted on 23
February 2026 to enhance students’ employability skills. The session provided practical
guidance on resume building, professional profiling, and preparation for recruitment
processes, benefiting 284 students.
A technical session on “Data Engineering Pipeline: From Ingestion and Storage to Analytics”
was delivered by Mr. Rajesh Korukonda, Technical Lead & Manager at Which Ltd., London, on
16 February 2026. The session provided insights into modern data engineering workflows and
analytics-driven decision-making, benefiting 200 participants.
Students of the department visited NRSC Training and Achievements, IDA Jeedimetla,
Hyderabad on 18 February 2026 as part of an industrial visit. A total of 149 students gained
practical exposure to real-world applications of AI, IoT, and remote sensing technologies.
The department achieved 100% pass percentage in courses handled by 34 faculty members,
reflecting the strong academic commitment and teaching effectiveness of the faculty.
The department’s placement initiatives recorded strong outcomes, achieving 86% placement
in the AIML program and 84% placement in the IoT program, demonstrating the effectiveness
of training and career development activities.
