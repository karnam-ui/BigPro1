Research Grant Approval
The faculty members of Vallurupalli Nageswara Rao Vignana Jyothi Institute of Engineering &
Technology (VNRVJIET), Hyderabad, have achieved a significant research milestone with the
approval of a collaborative sponsored research project titled “M-Pilot: Design and Development
of a Smart Living Companion with an IoT-Enabled Personal AI-Agent Leveraging In-Mobile
Metadata” under the ANRF-PAIR Scheme. The project has received a total sanctioned grant of
₹38,87,396, highlighting the quality and relevance of the proposed research. The project is being
carried out in collaboration with reputed institutions, fostering inter-institutional research and
innovation. Principal Investigators: Dr. Shailesh Kundalwal (Hub: IIT Indore), Dr. Umesh Kumar
Singh and Dr. Nischal Yadav (Spoke: Vikram University), Collaborative Investigators (VNRVJIET,
Hyderabad): Dr. Myneni Madhu Bala, Dr. Sabbineni Nagini and Dr.Thayyaba Khatoon Mohammed .
Funding Projects Applied
1.Autonomous Drone System for Solar Panel Fault Detection and Energy Loss Analytics
using AI and Thermal Vision- This project aims to develop an AI-enabled autonomous drone
system for identifying faults in solar panels using thermal vision. The system analyzes
energy losses and supports efficient maintenance of large-scale solar plants. It is led by Dr.
A. Brahmananda Reddy with Mr. Bhupesh Deka as Co-Investigator. The project is submitted
under the IMPACT 3.0 Program with a proposed funding of ₹20 Lakhs.
2.Smart Autonomous Drone System for Crop Stress, Pest & Yield Loss Prediction using AI and
Multispectral Imaging- The project focuses on precision agriculture through an autonomous
drone system integrated with AI and multispectral imaging. It enables early detection of
crop stress, pest infestation, and potential yield loss. The project is guided by Dr. A.
Brahmananda Reddy and Mr. Bhupesh Deka. It is proposed under IMPACT 3.0 with a funding
of ₹20 Lakhs.
3.Autonomous Drone System for Power Transmission Tower Fault Detection using AI and
Computer Vision- This project proposes an AI-based drone solution for automated
inspection of power transmission towers. Using computer vision techniques, the system
detects structural and component faults efficiently. The project is led by Mr. Bhupesh Deka
with Dr. A. Brahmananda Reddy as Co-Investigator. It is submitted to IMPACT 3.0 with a
proposed funding of ₹20 Lakhs.
