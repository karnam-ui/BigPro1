ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT
The Department was established in 2020 with the B.Tech. Data Science and Cyber Security
programmes with an intake of 60 in each programme. In 2021, B.Tech. AI & DS
programme started with an intake of 60, while the Data Science intake increased to 120
and later in the academic year 2023–24 the strength increased to 180. The Department
provides a Data Science minor degree, giving students a thorough grasp of fundamental
principles and practical skills in programming and statistical analysis.

FACULTY EXPERTISE
The faculty in the Data Science department is a formidable ensemble of seasoned professionals and
academicians with extensive expertise in the latest data science methodologies. Their collective knowledge spans
a diverse range of areas, including machine learning, statistical analysis, data visualization, and artificial
intelligence. With a commitment to staying abreast of industry trends, they bring real-world relevance to the
classroom, offering students invaluable insights and practical skills.
WHY B.TECH. CSE - DATA SCIENCE?
• Decision-Making
Data science provides valuable insights from large datasets, enabling informed and strategic decision-
making for businesses, organizations and governments.
•Predictive Analysis
Through statistical modeling and machine learning algorithms, data science enables the prediction of trends,
patterns, and future outcomes, aiding in proactive planning.
•Continuous Improvement
Through feedback loops and iterative analysis, data science supports continuous improvement in processes,
products, and services, fostering innovation and adaptability.
RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT
The Data Science department is a hub of innovative research, where faculty and students collaborate on cutting-
edge projects. From developing advanced machine learning algorithms to exploring novel data analysis
techniques, the department is at the forefront of pushing the boundaries of knowledge in the field. Research
works delve into diverse areas, including predictive modeling, natural language processing, and data-driven
decision-making.
The faculty members have published 209 papers in international journals, of
which 22 are in SCI, 2 are in WoS, 99 are in Scopus, and 79 are in UGC.
They have also published 7 national journals, of which 1 is Scopus and 6
are UGC listed.
The department has actively contributed to numerous patents, with 32
published and 1 granted. Faculty members participated in 81 international
conferences, including 5 in Web of Science (WoS) and 76 in Scopus.
Additionally, they engaged in 11 national conferences and contributed to
book chapters, with 2 in WoS and 4 in Scopus.
